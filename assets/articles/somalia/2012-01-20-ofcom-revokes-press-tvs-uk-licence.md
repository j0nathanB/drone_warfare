title: Ofcom revokes Press TV's UK licence
date: January 20, 2012
authors: Emma Slater, Chris Woods
content:

Britain's media regulator Ofcom determined that Press TV, an Iranian government-backed news channel, would be removed from UK airwaves effective immediately.

The channel faced removal after failing to meet two key regulatory requirements. First, it did not pay a £100,000 fine issued in December 2011 by the January deadline. This penalty stemmed from Press TV's concealment that a 2009 interviewee was being forcibly detained in Iran.

Second, Press TV declined to identify the person or entity controlling its UK operations on its broadcast licence.

Ofcom's press representative explained the action: "Editorial control does not sit with the UK licencee. We have given the broadcaster a number of opportunities to bring themselves into line, indeed we have bent over backwards to accommodate them, but they have failed to do so."

Press TV's leadership responded sharply to the decision. CEO Mohammad Sarafraz characterized the ruling as "an act of aggression by the British monarchy" that would deprive British citizens of truthful information.

The regulatory action followed a December 2011 Bureau investigation, published alongside The Guardian, revealing that Press TV had fabricated accounts of U.S. drone strikes in Somalia. The channel claimed 56 strikes killed approximately 1,370 civilians—figures the Bureau could not substantiate through independent verification.
