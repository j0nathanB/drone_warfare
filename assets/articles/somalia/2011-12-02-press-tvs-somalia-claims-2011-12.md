title: Press TV's Somalia Claims 2011-12
date: December 2, 2011
authors: Drones Team
content:

This investigative piece documents Iranian broadcaster Press TV's reporting on alleged US drone strikes in Somalia between July 2011 and October 2012. The Bureau compiled over 100 claims made by Press TV, noting that "in almost all of the more than 80 cases listed below, Press TV is the sole source of claims of an attack."

**Key Findings**

The investigation reveals that Press TV began reporting alleged US drone strikes thirteen days after the first confirmed strike on June 23, 2011. The station reported casualties ranging from individual incidents to mass casualty events, frequently claiming civilian deaths and injured parties.

Notable patterns include:
- Repeated claims of drone crashes across southern Somalia
- Casualty figures often lacking independent corroboration
- Reliance on anonymous Somali officials and tribal elders
- Claims of 30+ deaths in single incidents throughout the period

**Impact**

Following the Bureau's December 2, 2011 investigation, "Press TV's reporting of alleged strikes ceased for over a month, until January 6 2012." The Bureau continued monitoring Press TV claims through October 2012, documenting 108 separate reported incidents.

The dataset comprises detailed entries (PTV001-PTV108) listing dates, reported casualties, locations, and source references for verification purposes.
