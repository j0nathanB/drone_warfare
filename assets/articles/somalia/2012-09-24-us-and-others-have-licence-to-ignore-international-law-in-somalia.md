title: US and others have 'licence to ignore international law' in Somalia
date: September 24, 2012
authors: Jack Serle
content:

This investigative piece examines the extensive military presence of international forces operating in Somalia with minimal oversight or accountability.

**Key Claims:**

A Somali diplomat stated that foreign forces assisting against al Shabaab receive "a licence to completely ignore any local or international law." The Somali government lacks authority to question these operations.

**US Military Operations:**
The Bureau documented at least 10 US combat operations over five years. The US maintains approximately 2,500 military personnel in the Horn of Africa region, conducting covert operations since 2001 through Special Forces and armed drones. UN monitors recorded 64 unauthorized flights between June 2011 and April 2012, compared to only 12 reported by news agencies.

**Additional International Actors:**
- African Union peacekeeping forces (16,500 troops) trained by the Pentagon
- Ethiopian military incursions
- Private security contractors, including those associated with Blackwater founder Erik Dean Prince, training the Puntland Maritime Police Force
- Naval anti-piracy operations from multiple nations

**Accountability Gap:**
The article highlights that responsibility for civilian casualties remains unclear. No mechanism exists for oversight or accountability regarding foreign military actions in Somalia.
