title: First US drone strike in seven months hits Somalia
date: September 3, 2014
authors: Joseph Cox, Jack Serle
content:

A United States drone strike targeted Somalia in what marked the first such attack in seven months. The operation aimed to eliminate al Shabaab leader Ahmed Abdi Godane.

According to Abdullahi Abukar of the Somali Human Rights Association, the strike killed "six al Shabaab officers," though confirmation of Godane's death remained unclear. The attack destroyed an encampment and at least one vehicle in Lower Shabelle, an area under heavy al Shabaab control.

A Pentagon spokesman stated that "actionable intelligence led us to that site where we believe [Godane] was" and that the military believed it "hit what we were aiming at." He declined to confirm casualties. The operation involved drones and conventional aircraft piloted by US special forces, which deployed Hellfire and laser-guided missiles.

An al Shabaab spokesman and an unnamed Somali intelligence source both declined to confirm whether Godane was among those killed, with the latter suggesting Godane "might have been killed along with other militants."

This represented the sixth confirmed US drone strike in Somalia. Godane, also known as Mukhtar Abu Zubair, originally trained as an accountant before joining Itihad al Islamiya and later al Shabaab, becoming its leader in 2007. The US government offered up to $7 million for information about his location.

Godane had been targeted previously by US forces, including drone strikes in 2011 and earlier in 2014.
