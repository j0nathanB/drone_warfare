title: Somalia Targeted in US 'Drone War'
date: June 30, 2011
authors: Chris Woods
content:

A military drone operated by elite US Special Forces has targeted al Qaeda-linked militants in Somalia, killing several people in what marks "the first confirmed hostile use of drones in the east African country."

The June 23 strike in Kismayo, southern Somalia—initially reported as a helicopter attack—reportedly killed numerous foreign fighters and wounded two leaders of the al-Shabab militant group. These leaders allegedly maintained "direct ties" to US-born cleric Anwar al-Awlaki, based in Yemen and connected to al Qaeda operations there.

Bureau research indicates unmanned surveillance craft have operated over Somalia previously as part of broader military operations. In October 2009, a US drone was reportedly shot down in the country's south.

**Historical Context:**

US military operations against al-Shabab are conducted by the Joint Special Operations Command (JSOC), the same unit responsible for killing Osama bin Laden. JSOC maintains its own helicopter and drone fleets, plus access to Navy assets in the region.

The article documents multiple previous operations dating back to January 2007, including cruise missile strikes, gunship attacks, and helicopter raids. This drone strike represents Somalia becoming "the sixth recorded nation to be at the receiving end of US drone strikes"—following Afghanistan, Yemen, Iraq, Pakistan, and Libya.
