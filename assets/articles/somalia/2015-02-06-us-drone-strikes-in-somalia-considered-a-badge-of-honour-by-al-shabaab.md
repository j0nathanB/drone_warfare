title: Podcast: US drone strikes in Somalia considered a badge of honour by al Shabaab
date: February 6, 2015
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published a podcast episode featuring insights from BBC World Service journalists on US drone operations in Somalia and their unintended consequences.

**Key Themes:**

According to Mary Harper, BBC World Service Africa editor, American drone strikes paradoxically enhance al Shabaab's credibility. She explained that targeting the militant group with sophisticated technology "gives [al Shabaab] almost a legitimacy" and presents them as "a global force to be reckoned with."

Mohammed Mohammed, an editor at BBC World Service's Somali service, noted that American intelligence capabilities have improved. He stated: "it seems now that drones have become a force to be reckoned with."

**Intelligence Methods:**

Harper revealed that financial incentives drive intelligence collection, with informants receiving substantial payments—"$10,000 if not more"—for information about senior al Shabaab members.

**Humanitarian Impact:**

The strategy creates severe consequences for Somali civilians. Al Shabaab releases propaganda videos accusing alleged informants of planting tracking devices on members to guide drone strikes. These accused individuals are subsequently executed.

The episode highlighted tensions between counterterrorism objectives and civilian safety in conflict zones.
