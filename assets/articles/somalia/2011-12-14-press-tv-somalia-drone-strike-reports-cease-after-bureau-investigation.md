title: Press TV Somalia Drone Strike Reports Cease After Bureau Investigation
date: December 14, 2011
authors: Emma Slater
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published an investigation revealing that Iranian broadcaster Press TV had fabricated reports of U.S. drone strikes in Somalia. Between July and December 2011, Press TV claimed at least 1,370 people were killed in such attacks, with 749 alleged deaths in November alone across 28 reported strikes.

Following the Bureau's publication on December 2, 2011—in partnership with The Guardian—Press TV ceased reporting on drone strikes in Somalia entirely. The investigation found "no independent evidence to verify the reports," with a senior UN official stating that "Press TV is not a reliable source. It exaggerates and openly fabricates reports."

Within 12 days of publication, Press TV stopped its drone strike coverage completely and softened previous claims about civilian casualties. A Press TV producer named Barvasad denied the halt resulted from the Bureau's investigation, attributing it to lack of incoming reports. However, the timing suggests a direct correlation between the investigative exposé and the cessation of questionable reporting.

The article includes a graph documenting the sharp decline in Press TV's reported fatality and injury figures immediately following the Bureau's publication date.
