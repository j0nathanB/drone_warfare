title: Unprecedented death toll from US air strike in Somalia
date: March 7, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The US military conducted an airstrike on Raso Camp, a training facility for al Shabaab in central Somalia, approximately 100 miles north of Mogadishu. According to Pentagon statements, the operation resulted in approximately 150 fighter deaths, marking the highest single-strike casualty count recorded by the Bureau across all US counter-terrorism drone operations.

**Operation Details:**

The strike occurred on Saturday, March 5, 2016, using "manned and unmanned aircraft." US forces had monitored the camp for several weeks, believing it housed up to 200 al Shabaab operatives. Pentagon spokesman Captain Jeff Davis indicated the strike was authorized because the fighters "posed an imminent threat to US and African Union Mission in Somalia forces."

**Intelligence Coordination:**

Somalia's Foreign Minister Abdusalam Omer confirmed domestic involvement: "There has to be intelligence on the ground for this to happen. Our intelligence had helped."

**Historical Context:**

This represents an escalation in strike frequency, following ten attacks throughout 2015 in Somalia. The previous record casualty count from a single US counter-terrorism strike was 81 people killed in Pakistan in October 2006.

**Data Summary:**

- Reported strikes in Somalia (2001-2016): 19-23
- Total killed: 188-276
- Civilian casualties: 0-7
- Children killed: 0-2
