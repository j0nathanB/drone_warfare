title: US Accused of Killing 22 Somali Soldiers and Civilians
date: October 11, 2016
authors: Jessica Purkiss
content:

Somali officials alleged that a US airstrike on the city of Galkayo eliminated 22 regional soldiers and civilians. The US military confirmed conducting operations in the area but asserted that all casualties were members of al Shabaab.

According to US Africa Command, Somali forces were disrupting a bomb-making operation when attacked by al Shabaab fighters. The US intervened to "neutralize the threat." However, officials from the Galmudug region disputed this account, claiming the strike targeted their own forces and that rival Puntland officials falsely requested US support by misrepresenting the soldiers as terrorists.

The US has conducted counterterrorism operations in Somalia since 2001. Strike frequency has accelerated dramatically—15 operations occurred from 2007-2014, compared to 11 in 2015 alone and 15 within the first nine months of 2016.

Al Shabaab denied that any of its fighters or bases were in the targeted area. Somalia's government requested clarification from the US. Initially dismissing civilian casualty reports, the Pentagon subsequently announced an investigation after Secretary of Defense Ash Carter's announcement.
