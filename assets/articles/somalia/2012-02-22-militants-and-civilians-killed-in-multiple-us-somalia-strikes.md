title: Militants and civilians killed in multiple US Somalia strikes
date: February 22, 2012
authors: Chris Woods
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that between 9 and 21 U.S. military strikes in Somalia since 2007 have resulted in 51 to 169 deaths, with 11 to 59 identified as civilians. The investigation examined credible media reports, academic research, and military records spanning over a decade.

The research identifies three distinct phases of U.S. involvement. From 2001 through 2006, operations consisted primarily of covert surveillance and renditions. Beginning in December 2006, aerial and ground strikes intensified following Ethiopia's invasion. The third phase commenced in 2011, targeting al Shabaab directly.

**Key findings:**

- Nine attacks confirmed by U.S. officials killed 51-67 people (11-13 civilians)
- Four strikes reported by multiple credible sources killed 13-45 people (4-31 civilians)
- Eight strikes from single sources accounted for 22-57 deaths (15 civilian)

The Bureau verified fewer than one-third of reported strikes. U.S. Special Forces AC-130 gunships and drones operated from bases in Djibouti, Ethiopia, and possibly the Seychelles. The Pentagon declined providing clarifications on contested reports.

The study also debunked 56 Iranian Press TV claims of strikes allegedly killing 1,370 people, finding no corroborating evidence.
