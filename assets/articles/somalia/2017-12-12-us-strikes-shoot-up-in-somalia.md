title: US Strikes Shoot Up in Somalia
date: December 12, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported a dramatic escalation in American military operations in Somalia during November 2017. The organization documented that "US strikes in Somalia shot up in November, with American planes carrying out five times as many strikes as they did in the previous month."

The article notes that approximately half of these November strikes targeted the Islamic State group, marking the first instance of the U.S. directly targeting that organization in Somalia. A faction of fighters from Puntland pledged allegiance to ISIS in 2015, breaking from al Shabaab. According to UN monitoring bodies, this group expanded from dozens of fighters to approximately 200 members.

The increased operations reflected broader patterns. A total of 31 strikes hit Somalia throughout 2017, with one-third occurring in November alone. This represented a significant increase from the Obama administration's 14 strikes in 2016.

The article attributes this escalation partly to policy changes introduced by the Trump administration in March 2017. These rules designated certain Somali regions as "areas of active hostilities," granting military commanders greater operational flexibility to authorize strikes without additional oversight.
