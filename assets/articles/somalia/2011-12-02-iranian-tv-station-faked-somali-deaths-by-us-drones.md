title: Iranian TV Station 'Faked' Somali Deaths by US Drones
date: December 2, 2011
authors: Emma Slater, Chris Woods
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism exposed that Press TV, an Iranian state broadcaster, fabricated reports of US drone strikes in Somalia. The channel claimed over 1,370 people died in 56 alleged drone attacks since September 2011, with 383 categorized as civilians. However, Bureau research found no credible evidence supporting these casualty figures.

**Suspicious Patterns:**
The investigation revealed multiple red flags in Press TV's reporting. At least four reports were identical except for place names and numbers. Sources were named in only four of 56 reports. The Bureau could not verify individuals identified as "Somali military officials" or eyewitnesses despite contacting the Somali Government and Press TV's Tehran headquarters.

**Independent Verification:**
Representatives from the UN, African Union Mission in Somalia, NGOs, and journalists in Somalia could not confirm the strikes. A Somali charity director stated: "Press TV does have a penchant for exaggeration" and that casualty figures were "simply not possible."

A senior UN official characterized Press TV as unreliable, noting it "exaggerates and openly fabricates reports."

**Context:**
The first confirmed lethal US drone strike in Somalia occurred June 23, 2011. Press TV's reporting began in September 2011 with claims of civilian deaths, yet provided no photographic or video evidence.
