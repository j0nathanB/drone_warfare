title: Drone strikes are illegal according to a prominent Pakistani lawyer
date: 2014-01-16
authors: The Bureau
content: |
  The Bureau's podcast features an interview with Pakistani lawyer Ahmer Bilal Soofi, who addresses the legal status of drone strikes. According to Soofi, "drone strike victims should be able to seek compensation from 'the US government within the US'."

  Soofi contends the strikes violate both Pakistani sovereignty and international law, citing violations "by virtue of the constitution and also by virtue of the UN Charter." The interview was conducted by Owen Bennett-Jones in Islamabad.

  The article also covers related developments in drone operations:

  **Yemen Strike Investigation:** The US announced it would investigate a drone strike from December 12 that killed up to 15 civilians targeting vehicles heading to a wedding. This marks the first time the administration committed to investigating an operation outside Afghanistan's declared battlefield.

  **2014 Casualty:** Alice Ross reports on the first civilian casualty of 2014 from a strike in Yemen.

  **Pakistan Operation:** The piece discusses new reports that a German national was killed in a CIA drone strike in Pakistan in February 2012.

  The article accompanies a podcast episode titled "Drone News from the Bureau" and promotes a "Covert Drone War" newsletter subscription.
