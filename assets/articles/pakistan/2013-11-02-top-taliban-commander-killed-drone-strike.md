title: Top Taliban Commander Killed in US Drone Strike
date: 2013-11-02
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  Hakimullah Mehsud, leader of the Pakistan Taliban (TTP), was reported killed in a US drone strike, according to Taliban and security sources. The strike occurred in Pakistan's tribal areas and represented only the second drone attack there in 32 days.

  ## Key Details

  **About the Target:** Mehsud was described as "one of the most senior militants in Pakistan" and had led the TTP since August 2009. The US had offered a $5 million bounty for his capture. He was linked to the 2009 Camp Chapman bombing that killed seven CIA operatives and a failed 2010 New York car bombing attempt.

  **Impact on Peace Negotiations:** The strike came just hours after Pakistan's Prime Minister announced the government had begun peace talks with the Taliban—a claim the militant group initially disputed. Pakistan denounced the attack as "an attempt by the US to sabotage the negotiations."

  **Casualty Details:** His bodyguard and driver were also reportedly killed. Between five and 25 people were reported dead in the operation.

  **Leadership Succession:** Khan Said Sanja (aka Khalid) was reportedly elected as Mehsud's replacement, though some splinter groups questioned the legitimacy of his selection.
