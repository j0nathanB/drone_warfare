title: UK Complicity in US Drone Strikes is 'Inevitable', Emmerson Tells Parliament
date: 2013-12-05
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  UN expert Ben Emmerson QC and military analyst Professor Michael Clarke presented testimony to British lawmakers asserting that UK involvement in American drone operations is virtually certain.

  Emmerson stated that intelligence sharing between British and American agencies on drone targeting is "inevitable," noting that "It would be absurd if it were not the case." He framed this as an inherent feature of the UK-US intelligence relationship.

  Clarke emphasized the integration of information operations, asserting: "There's a reasonable presumption that the provision of information or sharing of information makes us complicit."

  The British government has maintained a position of neither confirming nor denying whether its intelligence agencies have provided information used in US drone strikes across Pakistan, Yemen, and Somalia—regions where the US has conducted over 430 covert drone operations outside declared war zones.

  Clarke noted that the UK has restricted its own drone use to declared conflict areas like Afghanistan, distinguishing British policy from American practices.

  The testimony highlighted an ongoing legal challenge brought by Pakistani citizen Noor Khan, whose father was killed in a 2011 CIA strike. Khan's case argues that British intelligence sharing could constitute complicity in potential war crimes.
