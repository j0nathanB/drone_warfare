title: Get the Data: What the Drones Strike
date: 2014-05-23
authors: Alice Ross, Jack Serle
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released a comprehensive dataset documenting targets reportedly struck by CIA drones in Pakistan. This collaborative project involved Forensic Architecture at Goldsmiths University and Situ Research in New York.

  ## Key Findings

  The research categorized drone strike targets into domestic buildings, religious institutions, vehicles, and other structures. The data indicated that "most US drone strikes in Pakistan attack houses," according to the accompanying story referenced in the article.

  ## Methodology

  Researchers extracted information from the Bureau's compiled strike reports, recording target types and cross-referencing multiple sources. Where reporting conflicted, they used minimum figures to account for uncertainties. The team acknowledged that "reporting is sometimes vague about the target, and sources sometimes directly contradict one other."

  ## Data Categories

  The dataset divided targets into:
  - Domestic buildings (often described as compounds)
  - Religious buildings (madrassas and mosques)
  - Vehicles (cars, trucks, motorcycles)
  - Other buildings (commercial, government structures)
  - Unclear targets

  The researchers also documented structural damage levels and incorporated casualty estimates alongside geographic coordinates based on reported locations.
