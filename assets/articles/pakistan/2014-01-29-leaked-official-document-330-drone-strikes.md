title: Leaked Official Document Records 330 Drone Strikes in Pakistan
date: 2014-01-29
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released a classified Pakistani government document detailing over 300 drone strikes in Pakistan between 2006 and September 2013. The records show locations, dates, and casualty estimates compiled by local Political Agents in the Federally Administered Tribal Areas (FATA).

  ## Key Findings

  **Total Deaths:** The document records approximately 2,217 deaths across the time period, closely matching the Bureau's independent count of at least 2,371 deaths.

  **Civilian Casualty Reporting Gap:** The most significant finding concerns a dramatic shift in how civilian deaths were documented. Until 2008, the file regularly noted civilian casualties in 15 of 37 drone strikes. After 2009, civilian deaths nearly vanish from the record—only seven of 294 subsequent incidents mention civilians, despite credible evidence of continued non-combatant casualties.

  **Notable Omission:** The October 2012 strike that killed 67-year-old Mamana Bibi is recorded as a single death with no mention of her civilian status or injured grandchildren, despite acknowledgment from Pakistani officials and documented compensation payments.

  ## Classification Issues

  Local officials categorized victims as "local" or "non-local" rather than "civilian" or "militant" to "avoid controversy," according to sources cited. The former FATA Secretariat official explained this approach prevented the political sensitivity of formally documenting civilian deaths.

  ## Expert Assessment

  Amnesty International researcher Mustafa Qadri stated: "The reliability of state records becomes questionable when well-documented cases go unrecorded," raising concerns about potential deliberate omission of civilian casualties following Barack Obama's 2009 inauguration.
