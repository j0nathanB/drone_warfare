title: Drone War Exposed: The Complete Picture of CIA Strikes in Pakistan
date: 2011-08-10
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published a comprehensive investigation revealing the scope of CIA drone operations in Pakistan. The research examined every recorded strike since 2004, presenting findings that contradicted official U.S. government claims about casualties.

  ## Key Findings

  The investigation documented:

  - **305 CIA drone strikes** in Pakistan since 2004—approximately 8% more than previously reported
  - **2,347 to 2,956 reported deaths** across all strikes
  - **175 children** identified in credible reports as killed
  - **392-781 civilians** reported dead, with at least 392 confirmed
  - **1,158+ people wounded** (first comprehensive injury accounting)
  - **253 strikes** authorized under President Obama—roughly one every four days

  ## Official Response

  A U.S. counter-terrorism official countered the findings, stating: "These actions target militants planning actively to kill Afghans, Pakistanis, Europeans, and Americans."

  The government claimed approximately 2,050 total deaths, asserting all but 50 were combatants.

  ## Reaction

  Amnesty International called for increased transparency, while Reprieve urged the public to question "official propaganda about drone warfare" and consider whether such operations were radicalizing populations.
