title: Al Qaeda Number Two Killed in Drone Strike
date: 2011-08-30
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The piece reports on the killing of Atiyah Abd al-Rahman, described as al Qaeda's second-in-command, in a CIA drone strike in Pakistan.

  According to the reporting, al-Rahman "had been second only to Ayman al-Zawahiri, al Qaeda's leader." The Libyan operative was reportedly targeted due to his documented links to terrorist networks in Iraq (discovered in 2006) and his suspected involvement in a 2009 suicide attack on a U.S. base in Afghanistan that killed seven CIA agents.

  U.S. officials indicated the strike occurred on August 22, targeting a house and vehicle near Mir Ali in North Waziristan, killing between four and seven individuals. However, the article notes that Pakistani outlet The News identified a possible second strike that day in Khaisoori that may have been responsible for al-Rahman's death, killing what was described as an Arab family.

  The timing followed Osama bin Laden's death by two months. The article also mentions that another al Qaeda figure, Ilyas Kashmiri, was eliminated in a June 3 drone strike, with his affiliated 313 Brigade subsequently appointing new leadership.
