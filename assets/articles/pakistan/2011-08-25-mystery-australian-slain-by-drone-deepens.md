title: Mystery of 'Australian' Slain by Drone Deepens
date: 2011-08-25
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism investigated a July 5 drone strike in Pakistan's tribal areas that killed a man identified as Saifullah or Saif Ullah. While initial reports suggested he was a 50-year-old Australian al Qaeda operative, the subsequent investigation deepened the mystery surrounding his nationality and affiliations.

  ### Key Findings

  **Australian Government Response**

  Australia's Foreign Affairs Ministry initially launched an investigation into the man's background. However, their conclusion contradicted earlier reporting: "There is no information to indicate an Australian was killed in the alleged attack."

  **Field Evidence**

  Bureau researchers in Waziristan provided contrasting evidence, stating: "There was no doubt about Saifullah's background. He was a white Australian convert who had grown a beard, and who had come to North Waziristan four years ago."

  **Actual Profile**

  Rather than being 50 years old and linked to al Qaeda, Saifullah was reportedly in his late 30s and connected to a militant group led by Hafiz Gul Bahadur. He regularly traveled between Pakistan's tribal areas and Khost, Afghanistan, fighting against Western and Afghan forces.

  ### Civilian Casualties

  The Bureau's broader investigation revealed that between 99 and 261 civilians were reportedly killed in Pakistan drone strikes between May 2010 and August 2011, contradicting U.S. claims of zero non-combatant deaths during that period.
