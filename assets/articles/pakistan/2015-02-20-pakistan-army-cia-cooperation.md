title: Pakistan Army Increasing CIA Cooperation on Drone Strikes
date: 2015-02-20
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that Pakistan's military has resumed collaboration with the CIA on drone operations following leadership changes and the December 2014 Taliban attack on a military school in Peshawar.

  According to Dr. Ayesha Siddiqa, an Islamabad-based defense analyst interviewed for the podcast Drone News, Pakistan's rulers shifted their previously oppositional stance. She explained that the new army leadership became willing to assist American drone strikes targeting Taliban elements threatening Pakistan, describing it as addressing "a common problem."

  The arrangement resembles earlier cooperation under General Pervez Musharraf—an informal understanding where Pakistan provides intelligence in exchange for strikes aligned with its interests. However, Siddiqa noted this current arrangement involves "better" concessions to Afghan and US governments.

  Dr. Humeria Iqtidar from King's College London contributed analysis regarding Pakistani perceptions of Pashtun populations, suggesting stereotyping as "primitive people" has intensified over recent years, conflating Pashtun identity with Taliban and tribal affiliations. She also noted internal military disconnects, with rank-and-file soldiers experiencing "immense cynicism and despair" regarding their mission.

  The article includes references to related drone warfare investigations by the Bureau's team.
