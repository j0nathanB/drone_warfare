title: The Reaper Presidency: Obama's 300th Drone Strike in Pakistan
date: 2012-12-03
authors: Alice Ross, Chris Woods, Sarah Leo
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism documented that a December 1, 2012 drone strike in Shin Warsak, South Waziristan marked President Barack Obama's 300th such operation in Pakistan. The attack reportedly killed Abdul Rehman al-Zaman Yemeni and up to three others. During Obama's first term, CIA strikes occurred roughly once every five days—six times the frequency under George W. Bush.

  ## Key Timeline Highlights

  **2009:** Obama's drone campaign launched just three days into his presidency, with initial strikes killing at least 12 civilians including four children. By year's end, at least 119 civilians had reportedly died. Notable tactics included two funeral strikes, with the second occurring eight days after Obama received the Nobel Peace Prize.

  **2010:** The CIA responded to the Khost bombing with intensified strikes. The Bureau found that "one in three reportedly killed civilians." Pakistan's growing resentment culminated in December when a CIA station chief's cover was blown in a lawsuit brought by a drone victim.

  **2011:** Tensions escalated dramatically. After Osama bin Laden's May 1 raid, the CIA intensified bombing despite John Brennan's claims of zero civilian casualties. The Bureau subsequently released data showing "at least 385 civilians including 168 children" had been killed. November brought a critical rupture: Pakistan expelled U.S. drones from Shamsi air base after a NATO strike killed 24 Pakistani soldiers. December saw zero reported strikes.

  **2012:** After initial diplomatic efforts by Vice President Joe Biden and Secretary of State Hillary Clinton, Obama publicly acknowledged the CIA campaign in January. He stated drones were "precision strikes against al Qaeda and their affiliates," though reporting suggested civilians continued dying. The program remained politically unopposed, with Republican nominee Mitt Romney stating he supported "entirely" the policy.

  ## Civilian Impact

  Academic studies documented that constant drone presence caused "suffering and distress," hardening anti-American sentiment among affected populations. The UN announced a special investigation unit to examine civilian casualties, with leaders referring to funeral and rescuer targeting as potentially constituting war crimes.
