title: US drones appear to have returned to Pakistan
date: March 6, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported that the Trump administration conducted a drone strike in Pakistan on March 2, 2017, marking the resumption of operations after an extended pause.

**Key Details:**

The strike targeted the Kurram tribal area near the Afghanistan border, killing two individuals identified as Qari Abdullah Sabari (an Afghan Taliban commander) and Shakir (an Afghan national). Two missiles struck their motorcycle.

**Significance:**

This represented the first U.S. drone strike in Pakistan since May 2016—the longest gap between strikes since operations began in 2004. The Bureau recorded "425 drone strikes in Pakistan since 2004," with estimates of "424-966 civilians killed."

**Context:**

Pakistani lawyer Shahzad Akbar from Reprieve commented that this was "President Trump's first drone strike in Pakistan," suggesting "drones have returned to the tribal areas." The 2013 Peshawar High Court had declared such strikes violated Pakistani law and sovereignty.

**Official Response:**

U.S. Central Command denied involvement; the CIA declined to comment. Conflicting reports emerged regarding whether the strike occurred in Pakistan or Afghanistan.
