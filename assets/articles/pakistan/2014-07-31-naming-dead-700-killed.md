title: Naming the Dead Project Records Names of Over 700 Killed by Drones in Pakistan
date: 2014-07-31
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism's "Naming the Dead" project has documented more than 700 individuals reportedly killed by CIA drone strikes in Pakistan. The database represents a significant documentation effort, though these identified individuals comprise fewer than one-third of approximately 2,342 total reported deaths from drone attacks in Pakistan's northern tribal areas.

  ## Key Findings

  **Casualty Breakdown:**
  - Over 700 names recorded
  - 323 identified as civilians (nearly half)
  - 99 identified children among civilian deaths
  - 295 identified militants
  - 95 classified as "unknown" status

  The Bureau notes a critical discrepancy: "The US authorities claim that only militants are killed in drone strikes," yet their research suggests significant civilian casualties remain unidentified.

  **Project Scope:**
  Launched in September 2013, the project compiled 568 names from over 370 strikes since 2004. It represents the largest publicly accessible list of drone victims, drawing from media reports, court documents, eyewitness accounts, and leaked Pakistani government materials.

  **Gender Disparity:**
  Only two women have been identified by name despite estimates that at least 55 women died in the decade-long drone campaign, reflecting underreporting in the region's conservative cultural context.
