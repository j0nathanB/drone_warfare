  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism maintains a database of nearly 300 Pakistani drone strikes spanning from 2004 through publication. While compiling detailed statistics—casualties, civilians killed, locations, dates—into numerical form reveals patterns and trends, it risks obscuring "the brutal reality of individual events."

  ## Three Notable Strikes

  **B47 – Azam Warsak (December 11, 2008)**
  One of the final strikes under President George W. Bush killed seven people in South Waziristan. Reports suggested three may have been Punjabi militants, though villagers claimed all victims were civilians. A local religious scholar condemned the attacks, stating the drones "have deprived the innocent tribesmen of their mental peace."

  **Ob167 – Khushali (December 6, 2010)**
  A drone targeted a car carrying alleged militants in North Waziristan. When one suspect fled into a nearby shop, the drone fired two additional missiles, killing the target but also two civilians—illustrating how "determination to kill militant targets appears to escalate into indiscriminate behaviour."

  **Ob219 – Karez (June 15, 2011)**
  One of only two strikes resulting in no deaths or injuries. A missile fired at a moving vehicle with four alleged militants overshot completely. Rather than pursuing fleeing occupants, the drone destroyed the abandoned vehicle.
