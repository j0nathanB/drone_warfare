title: Human Rights Groups Say Civilian Drone Deaths Violate International Law
date: 2013-10-22
authors: Rachel Oldroyd
content: |
  Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch released investigations documenting civilian casualties from drone strikes in Pakistan and Yemen, arguing these killings breach international law and calling for U.S. accountability.

  ## Key Findings

  **Pakistan Strikes:**
  Amnesty's report identified 18 laborers, including a 14-year-old boy, killed in a July 2012 attack. The organization stated: "This attack violated the prohibition of the arbitrary deprivation of life and may constitute war crimes."

  The report also documented the October 2012 death of Mamana Bibi, a 68-year-old grandmother killed near her grandchildren. Researchers found no credible evidence justifying the strike despite claims of nearby militant activity.

  **Yemen Strikes:**
  Human Rights Watch examined six strikes killing 82 people, including at least 57 civilians. One incident involved a passenger van strike that killed 12 civilians, including the family of 23-year-old Ahmad al-Sabooli.

  ## Demands for Transparency

  Both organizations emphasized that secrecy surrounding drone operations prevents victims' families from obtaining justice or compensation. The reports urged the U.S. government to publicly acknowledge strikes and investigate all civilian casualties.

  Human rights experts from the UN also called for greater disclosure and transparency regarding drone operations.
