title: CIA Drones Kill at Least 13 in Pakistan: The Bloodiest Strike for More Than a Year
date: 2014-07-16
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  A US drone strike in the Datta Khel area of Pakistan killed at least 13 people in what represented the deadliest attack in over a year. The operation destroyed a house and vehicle early Wednesday morning, marking the second strike in six days targeting the region.

  According to intelligence sources, the compound housed foreign militants and local terrorists. The victims remained unidentified, though reports suggested up to 12 were Uzbeks. This strike deviated from recent patterns showing an average of four deaths per operation.

  The attack occurred amid Pakistan's military offensive in North Waziristan, Operation Zar i Azb, which aimed to clear armed groups from the region. Datta Khel has endured sustained targeting, with at least 35 drone strikes hitting the area since 2004, resulting in approximately 109 deaths, including roughly 50 civilians.

  The broader context included over one million people displaced from North Waziristan, with more than 990,000 registered in refugee camps. The article notes that Pakistani air strikes had also conducted operations in the region, with Bureau investigations documenting 291-540 deaths across 15 strikes over six months preceding ground operations.
