title: Podcast: Inside the Bureau's Latest Drones Investigation
date: 2014-05-23
authors: Bureau Reporter
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released a podcast discussing new research on CIA drone operations in Pakistan. Reporter Alice K Ross and host Owen Bennett-Jones examine findings from an investigation into targeting patterns over a decade-long period.

  **Key Findings:**

  The research reveals that "more than 60% of all CIA drone strikes in Pakistan targeted domestic buildings," with at least 222 civilians reportedly killed in these strikes. Notably, this pattern persisted throughout the entire 10-year drone war period studied.

  **Research Collaboration:**

  The analysis was conducted jointly with Forensic Architecture at Goldsmiths, University of London, and Situ Research in New York. The data appears on an interactive map titled "Where the Drones Strike."

  **Notable Observations:**

  Susan Schuppli from Forensic Architecture noted that "domestic buildings are in fact the primary targets," contradicting perceptions that strikes had shifted away from residential structures toward vehicles over time.

  The podcast also covered UK Reaper drones announced in December 2010 that remained non-operational despite being intended to support British troops in Afghanistan.
