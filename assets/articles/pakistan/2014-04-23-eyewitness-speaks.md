title: 'I saw a big balloon of dust. Then I heard a bang' – an eyewitness speaks
date: 2014-04-23
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  An eyewitness named Noor recounted his experience observing a drone strike in South Waziristan, Pakistan, roughly four years before the interview. He described watching from behind his house as a drone fired a missile at a vehicle traveling below.

  In Noor's account, the initial strike wounded the occupants, who attempted to flee but "couldn't walk properly." A second drone attack followed, resulting in what he characterized as fatal injuries to all three individuals. Local residents, including Noor, collected bodies before a large contingent of militants—estimated between 50-60 people—arrived to claim the remains.

  The article identifies the vehicle's suspected owner as "Asmaray Khan," a feared Arab commander. Through investigation, the author discovered this was a nickname for **Usama al Kini**, a Kenya-born senior al Qaeda member with a $5 million FBI bounty.

  However, a significant chronological discrepancy emerged. Official records indicated al Kini died in a January 1, 2009 strike on an abandoned school, involving a bunker-busting bomb. Noor's description—mentioning two missiles and a vehicle strike—more closely matched an attack the previous day, December 31, 2008, targeting a Peugeot with three occupants.

  The piece concludes by highlighting the challenges in verifying eyewitness accounts, even from credible sources, as details may contain substantial errors or conflate multiple events.
