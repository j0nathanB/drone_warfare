title: Witnesses Speak Out
date: 2012-02-04
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism presents eyewitness accounts from Waziristan describing CIA drone strikes that killed both combatants and civilians, including rescue workers and funeral attendees.

  ## Key Incidents

  **Degan Village (December 17, 2009)**

  Zahidullah witnessed the aftermath of attacks targeting al Qaeda commanders. He described seeing "a total of 16 people died in these attacks of which six were civilian rescuers and two Taliban rescuers." Following initial strikes, drones returned as villagers attempted rescue operations, killing additional people.

  **Danda Darpakhel (September 16, 2010)**

  Journalist Samiullah Khan was interviewing a Taliban commander when drones struck nearby compounds. After the initial attack killed many, rescuers responded. Khan recounted: "the drones returned and fired again" when people arrived to help, causing additional casualties among civilians and Taliban members alike.

  **Ghulam Khan (December 28, 2010)**

  Witness Ihsanullah Khan described successive strikes on two nearby homes that killed both Taliban fighters and civilians.

  **Funeral Strike (June 23, 2009)**

  CIA drones struck a funeral attended by 5,000 people, killing up to 83 individuals. Washington Post correspondent Joby Warrick revealed that U.S. intelligence sources indicated the funeral attack was deliberate—designed to eliminate Taliban leadership after initially targeting a commander.

  ## Pattern Analysis

  Warrick explained that CIA planners viewed strikes as "creating opportunities" to target additional high-value individuals, with limited consideration of civilian casualties beyond attempting to avoid women and children.
