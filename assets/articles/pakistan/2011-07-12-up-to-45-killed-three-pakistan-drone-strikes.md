title: 'Up to 45 killed' in three Pakistan drone strikes
date: 2011-07-12
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  Three separate drone strikes in Pakistan's Federally Administered Tribal Area occurred between Tuesday evening and early Wednesday morning, resulting in significant casualties. According to the Bureau's analysis, these attacks represent part of a broader pattern, with "229 CIA drone strikes" having taken place in Pakistan since January 2009.

  The first strike targeted "a van and compound on Tuesday evening in Borvaika or Gorwak village in North Waziristan," killing five alleged militants in the vehicle and between 12-15 in a nearby house, with six injured.

  A second attack in Bushnarai, South Waziristan, destroyed a compound and/or vehicle, with estimates of 6-10 alleged militants killed and five injured. Reports indicated foreign nationals may have been among the deceased.

  A third strike in the Shawal area of North Waziristan targeted a house and car, with death estimates ranging from 13-15 people and numerous injured.

  The article notes reporting challenges from tribal areas and emphasizes uncertainty surrounding precise casualty figures across all three incidents.
