title: Drone strike hits tribal areas as US-Pakistan tensions rise
date: September 18, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss
content:

A U.S. drone strike on September 15 killed three men in the tribal area of Kurram, located on the Afghanistan border, according to government officials, security sources, and tribal representatives.

This strike marks the first operation following President Trump's announcement of a new Afghanistan strategy. In his remarks, Trump stated he would "no longer be silent about Pakistan's safe havens for terrorist organisations," raising concerns about potential escalation of drone operations in the region.

The strike targeted a compound belonging to Maulvi Mohib, reportedly affiliated with the Haqqani Network according to Afghan Taliban sources. The Haqqani Network is implicated in numerous Afghan attacks, including a significant truck bombing in Kabul on May 31.

This represents the fifth strike in 2017. Operations resumed in March following a nine-month pause, though strikes have been sporadic. One June strike in Hangu's settled areas—only the third outside tribal zones in 429 total strikes since 2004—drew Pakistan's military chief's ire. He demanded the U.S. share intelligence and warned that unilateral actions were "counterproductive."

U.S.-Pakistan tensions continue escalating as the Trump administration explores hardening its Pakistan approach, potentially expanding drone programs.
