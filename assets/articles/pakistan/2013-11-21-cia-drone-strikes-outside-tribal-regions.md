title: CIA drone strikes outside Pakistan's tribal regions
date: 2013-11-21
authors: Alice Ross, Jack Serle
content: |
  A CIA drone strike targeted a religious school in Hangu, Pakistan, marking what was believed to be the first attack in Pakistan's "settled" areas outside the tribal regions. The pre-dawn strike killed at least six people and wounded several others.

  ## Key Details

  The attack hit a madrassa in a village called Tal near Hangu in Khyber-Pakhtunkhwa Province. The mud building had 15 rooms, though missiles struck only one. Around 80 students escaped unharmed. Residents reported seeing drones circling the area for days prior to the strike.

  ## Significance

  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism had documented over 370 drone strikes in Pakistan since 2004, with almost all occurring in the Federally Administered Tribal Area (FATA). This strike represented a significant expansion of the target zone into settled provincial areas.

  ## Intended Targets

  The operation appeared focused on the Haqqani Network. Among those reportedly killed was Ahmad Jan, described as a senior Haqqani commander and head of the shadow finance ministry for Paktika province in Afghanistan. As many as five additional senior Haqqani commanders were allegedly killed.

  ## Political Context

  The strike occurred one day after Pakistan's foreign adviser assured parliament that the U.S. had agreed to suspend drone operations during peace negotiations with the Taliban, heightening tensions regarding U.S. commitment to those talks.
