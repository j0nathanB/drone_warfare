title: Imran Khan's March Brings Global Attention to CIA Drone Strikes
date: 2012-10-11
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  Pakistani politician Imran Khan led a two-day protest march into Pakistan's tribal region to spotlight CIA drone operations. The convoy, which included US peace activists, international journalists, and thousands of local participants, aimed to reach Kotkai in Waziristan but was ultimately redirected.

  ## Key Events

  The march departed Islamabad on Saturday with an estimated 15km-long convoy. Despite Taliban threats and government roadblocks, participants pushed forward. However, delays caused by authorities blocking routes with shipping containers prevented the group from reaching Waziristan. The rally was held outside Tank instead, drawing tens of thousands.

  ## Notable Participants

  - **Clive Stafford Smith** (Reprieve): "The real goal was to provoke discussion of the drone issue, and that goal was reached on Saturday"
  - **Medea Benjamin** (Code Pink): Emphasized the march demonstrated American opposition to drone policies
  - **Shahzad Akhbar** (human rights lawyer): Noted the event successfully highlighted the issue internationally

  ## Diplomatic Meeting

  Activists presented a petition signed by 3,000 people—including filmmaker Oliver Stone and author Alice Walker—to US Ambassador Richard Hoagland, citing Bureau data showing at least 474 civilian deaths from drone strikes.

  According to activists, Hoagland stated the US conducts "no deliberate strikes against civilian rescuers," marking rare public US commentary on this tactic.
