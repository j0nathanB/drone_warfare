title: Australian 'al Qaeda boss' killed in drone strike
date: 2011-07-07
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  A CIA drone strike on July 5, 2011, reportedly killed Saifullah, an alleged Australian jihadist described as a middle-ranking al Qaeda leader. The strike targeted a guesthouse near the main bazaar in Mir Ali, North Waziristan, Pakistan, around 11 PM local time, resulting in six reported deaths and five wounded.

  The Bureau's analysis noted this represented one of only a handful of Western jihadists killed in US drone operations over the preceding year, amid hundreds of casualties among Pakistani and Afghan suspected militants.

  The article also reported two additional drone strikes in southern Somalia on the same evening, targeting "militant camps" in Dhoobley and Afmadow, with reports indicating "dozens" killed. Somalia had recently become the sixth nation hosting US armed drone operations.

  An Australian Foreign Affairs Ministry spokesperson confirmed awareness of reports regarding Saifullah's alleged connection and stated that "relevant agencies are currently investigating the alleged Australian connection."

  The piece noted this would be the second al Qaeda commander killed by CIA drones in Pakistan within four weeks, following confirmation that senior leader Ilyas Kashmiri died in a June 3 strike.
