title: Pakistani Court Rules CIA Drone Strikes Are Illegal
date: 2013-05-09
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  A Peshawar High Court judge delivered a landmark ruling declaring CIA drone strikes in Pakistan to be illegal. Chief Justice Dost Muhammad Khan determined that the strikes constitute "criminal offences," "war crimes," and violations of fundamental human rights, resulting in hundreds of civilian deaths.

  The court ordered Pakistan's government to "use force if need be" to halt drone operations in tribal regions and to formally demand the United States cease the campaign. Khan also called for UN Security Council intervention and instructed Pakistani authorities to compile casualty data and provide compensation to victims.

  The case was brought by the Foundation for Fundamental Rights on behalf of Noor Khan, whose father died in a March 2011 strike targeting tribal elders. The ruling came two days before national elections and presented a test for the incoming government regarding compliance.

  **Key statement from lawyer Shahzad Akbar:** "It is a landmark judgment: drone victims in Waziristan will now get some justice after a long wait."

  The judge clarified that drone strikes would remain unlawful regardless of whether Pakistani officials secretly authorized them. Scholar Naureen Shah emphasized that the ruling increases pressure on the US to address civilian casualty claims through investigations and public disclosure.
