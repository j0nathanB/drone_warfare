title: Bureau Investigation: CIA Drone Strikes on Rescuers
date: 2013-08-01
authors: Chris Woods, Mushtaq Yusufzai
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism's field investigation in Pakistan's tribal areas documents evidence that the CIA briefly revived "double-tap" strikes—deliberately targeting rescuers at drone strike scenes—in mid-2012. The tactic had previously been labeled a potential war crime by UN investigators.

  ## Key Findings

  The investigation focused on strikes in North Waziristan aimed at al Qaeda operative Yahya al-Libi, who was killed June 4, 2012. Researchers identified five double-tap strikes occurring between May and July 2012, resulting in 53 deaths and 57 injuries according to field sources.

  **Notable incidents:**
  - May 24: Mosque strike in Hasukhel, followed by targeting of six rescuers
  - June 4: Al-Libi's death in a sequence of strikes killing approximately 16 people
  - July 6 & 23: Additional strikes on rescuers, with potential civilian casualties

  ## Congressional Oversight Discrepancy

  CIA video reportedly showed only al-Libi dying to congressional oversight committees. However, media reports and field research indicated significantly higher death tolls with rescuers deliberately targeted. The CIA stated it "provides accurate and timely information" to oversight committees.

  ## Legal Implications

  UN rapporteurs characterized intentional targeting of civilian rescuers as potential "war crimes." However, legal experts note secondary strikes may be lawful if targeting legitimate military personnel, provided proper precautions are taken.
