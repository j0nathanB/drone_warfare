title: Secret US Documents Show Brennan's 'No Civilian Drone Deaths' Claim Was False
date: 2013-04-11
authors: Jack Serle, Chris Woods
content: |
  Leaked US intelligence documents contradict public statements made by John Brennan, then President Obama's counterterrorism adviser, regarding civilian casualties in Pakistan drone strikes.

  In June 2011, Brennan claimed that for "almost a year" no civilians had died in US drone operations in Pakistan. However, documents obtained by McClatchy news agency revealed that at least one civilian death occurred on April 22, 2011—two months before Brennan's statement.

  ## Key Findings

  **The April 22 Strike:** A dawn attack killed approximately 25 people in North Waziristan. Seven media organizations reported at least five civilian deaths, including three children. Both the Associated Press and TBIJ confirmed civilian casualties through field investigations, though US officials denied such claims at the time.

  **Discrepancies in Official Accounts:** Throughout 2011, US intelligence sources insisted civilians were no longer being killed. On June 29, Brennan stated there had been no civilian deaths in 10 months—a claim TBIJ challenged after submitting documentation of 45 civilian deaths during the referenced period.

  **ISI-CIA Cooperation:** The documents revealed deeper collaboration between Pakistan's ISI and the CIA than previously known, including instances where the ISI granted "forced approval" for strikes after CIA pressure.

  **Bush-Era Operations:** The documents also documented civilian deaths from 2008 operations, including women and children from the Haqqani family killed in a September 2008 strike targeting the network's patriarch.
