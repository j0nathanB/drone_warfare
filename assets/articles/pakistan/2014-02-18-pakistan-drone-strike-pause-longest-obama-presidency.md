title: Pakistan Drone Strike Pause is the Longest of Obama's Presidency
date: 2014-02-18
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  The CIA had not conducted a drone strike in Pakistan for 55 days as of the article's publication—marking the longest pause during President Obama's tenure. The last recorded attack occurred on December 25, 2013, near Miranshah in North Waziristan.

  ## Key Points

  **Strike Activity:**
  - The CIA executed 330 attacks in Pakistan under Obama
  - No strikes occurred in 2014 through mid-February
  - Strike frequency declined from a 2010 peak, averaging one attack every two weeks in 2013

  **Possible Reasons for Pause:**
  The timing coincided with peace negotiations between Pakistan's government and the Pakistan Taliban (TTP). US officials told the Washington Post that drone operations had been "sharply curtailed" at Islamabad's request to facilitate talks, though a White House official later disputed this characterization.

  **Uncertainty About Motivations:**
  An Amnesty USA advocate noted the lack of transparency: "It's absurd that this many years into the drone programme we still get most of our news about it from conflicting leaks" regarding whether stricter targeting standards or diplomatic considerations drove the pause.

  **Future Operations:**
  Despite the hiatus, media reports indicated Washington was considering targeting a US citizen reportedly located in Pakistan, suggesting the campaign might not be permanently concluded.
