title: Two boys reported killed in CIA strike
date: 2011-11-02
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that two boys—16-year-old Tariq Khan and his 12-year-old cousin Wahid—were killed in a CIA drone strike in Pakistan on October 31, 2011. The strike targeted a vehicle near Mir Ali in Waziristan, killing up to four people total.

  ## Key Details

  Tariq Khan had attended an anti-drone rally in Islamabad just days before his death. He participated in a "Waziristan Grand Jirga" organized by the UK charity Reprieve and Pakistani partners on October 28, 2011, where he heard discussions about ending civilian suffering from drone strikes.

  According to Shahzad Akbar, a Pakistani lawyer, Tariq was "one of the potential camera trainees we hoped would film the activity of drones in Waziristan. He was young and very enthusiastic."

  ## Statistical Context

  The Bureau's data documented between 111 and 281 civilians reportedly killed in US drone strikes between May 1, 2010, and the date of this strike. Sixteen of those were children. This attack brought the total of children reported killed in US drone strikes since 2004 to 175.

  The CIA declined to comment on whether it maintained its position of having killed no civilians since May 2010.
