title: In lawless lands: Amnesty charts human rights abuses in Pakistan's tribal belt
date: 2012-12-19
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  The article examines an Amnesty International report documenting severe human rights violations in Pakistan's Federally Administered Tribal Areas (FATA), particularly Waziristan, a region that has experienced over 350 drone strikes according to the Bureau's count.

  ### Key Points:

  **Government and Military Abuses:**
  The report details how Pakistan's army conducts unlawful detentions without due process, including torture and extrajudicial killings. Amnesty documented 39 cases of enforced disappearances linked to the military, though officials indicate potentially thousands remain in undisclosed custody. Victims describe being detained on weak evidence of Taliban connections and subjected to beatings and torture.

  **Taliban Violence:**
  The militant group conducts summary trials targeting those accused of espionage, supporting the government, or promoting "un-Islamic" agendas like women's rights. These proceedings frequently result in executions without meeting international justice standards. The group deliberately executes alleged informants and mutilates bodies to deter cooperation with authorities.

  **Systemic Impunity:**
  Amnesty attributes the widespread abuses to the Pakistani government's failure to extend justice protections to tribal areas, creating "total impunity" for both military and militant forces. The organization warns that without urgent government intervention, the region's three million civilians remain "locked in a perpetual state of lawlessness."

  Human Rights activists, journalists, and humanitarian workers face particular danger from militants in the region.
