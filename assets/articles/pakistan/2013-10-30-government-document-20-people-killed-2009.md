title: Government Document Identifies 20 People Killed in Pakistan in 2009 Drone Strike
date: 2013-10-30
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  The Bureau obtained a Pakistani government document naming 20 individuals killed in a March 12, 2009 drone strike on a house in Barjo, Kurram Agency. This work was part of the Bureau's "Naming the Dead" project, which aims to identify drone strike victims in Pakistan's tribal areas.

  ## Key Details

  **The Strike:** Multiple missiles hit a residence reportedly used by Pakistan Taliban commander Fazal Saeed Haqqani, who survived the attack. Initial media reports suggested 14-26 deaths, with some sources indicating civilian casualties.

  **The Document:** Created by Pakistani local government officials, the list identifies 20 locals killed and six injured, including their tribes, villages, and fathers' names, though it did not provide ages.

  **Eyewitness Accounts:** Two local witnesses independently identified victims from the document but provided varying casualty figures. One stated "12 were killed. Three women and two children were among the dead," while another described "almost 12 people including three boys and three girls."

  **Notable Victims:** Identified individuals included Ahmed Said, age 22-23, a Peshawar University student, and Zahid Rehman, 21, described as a well-liked college graduate.

  **Context:** The Bureau's broader research suggests over 2,500 people died in Pakistani drone strikes, with only 614 individuals identified by the project's launch date.
