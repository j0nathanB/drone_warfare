title: CIA Drone Strikes Resume in Pakistan After Five-Month Pause
date: 2014-06-12
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  Two drone strikes hit North Waziristan in Pakistan's tribal northwest, reportedly killing 16 people and marking the end of the longest pause in CIA drone operations during President Obama's administration.

  The strikes resumed after a five-month hiatus that began on Christmas Day. Pakistani authorities had requested the US halt drone operations to facilitate peace talks with the Pakistani Taliban (TTP). However, following an attack on Karachi airport by the TTP and the Islamic Movement of Uzbekistan that killed at least 39 people, the peace negotiations effectively collapsed.

  ## Strike Details

  **First Strike:** A vehicle and houses in a village west of Miranshah were targeted, reportedly killing up to six people. Local reports suggested the dead included Uzbek militants and members of either the Punjabi Taliban or Haqqani Network.

  **Second Strike:** Hours later, drones attacked again in the early hours of Thursday, killing at least 10 alleged militants. Multiple reports indicated the second strike targeted the same location as the first, with some sources claiming drones fired on people attempting rescue operations—a tactic previously documented by the Bureau.

  ## Government Response

  Pakistan's foreign ministry condemned the strikes as violations of sovereignty. However, unnamed "top government officials" told Reuters that the strikes had "express approval of the Pakistan government and army," indicating coordination between US and Pakistani authorities.
