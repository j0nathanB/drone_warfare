title: Bureau Podcast Interviews Drone Strike Eyewitness
date: 2014-01-09
authors: Bureau Reporter
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released a podcast episode featuring an interview with an eyewitness from Pakistan's tribal region who described witnessing a CIA drone strike in 2009.

  The unnamed witness, whose identity was protected for safety reasons, recounted to interviewer Owen Bennett-Jones what he observed during an attack in South Waziristan. He stated: "I saw a big balloon of dust. Then I heard a bang."

  According to the account, a vehicle and its occupants came under attack. After the initial strike, three men exited the damaged car, appearing injured. A second missile struck the men moments later. The witness identified one casualty as a commander known locally as Asmaraykhan.

  Alice K Ross, leading the Bureau's drone investigations team, compared the eyewitness testimony against the organization's database of Pakistani drone strikes. The researchers believe the incident corresponds to a strike occurring on January 1, 2009—the penultimate operation of George W. Bush's presidency.

  The podcast episode also covered Pentagon developments regarding unmanned systems and additional drone-related news developments.
