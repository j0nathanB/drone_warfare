title: Get the data: Twenty-five deadly strikes
date: 2011-07-18
authors: Chris Woods, Rahimullah Yusufzai
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism investigated 116 US drone strikes in Pakistan between August 23, 2010 and June 29, 2011. The investigation challenged claims by the Obama administration that no civilians had been killed in drone operations during this period.

  ## Key Findings

  The Bureau identified **25 strikes where civilian deaths likely occurred**. According to their analysis:

  - At least **10 strikes definitively involved civilian casualties**
  - A minimum of **45 civilians died** in these confirmed cases
  - Six victims were children under 16 years old
  - An additional 15 strikes raised significant questions, with at least 65 more civilian deaths reported but contested or single-sourced

  ## Methodology

  Researchers reviewed thousands of reports covering over 280 strikes spanning seven years. They:

  - Worked with lawyers and researchers representing reportedly killed civilians
  - Employed researchers in Waziristan to corroborate evidence
  - Gathered photographs from human rights organizations and local journalists
  - Consulted Pakistani intelligence officers, medical personnel, and local sources

  ## Notable Cases

  The article documented ten strikes with strong evidence of civilian deaths, including:

  - **September 8, 2010:** Four children killed in Danda Darpakhel
  - **October 18, 2010:** Ten-year-old Naeem Ullah killed by shrapnel
  - **March 17, 2011:** Between 19-30 civilians died at a tribal council meeting in Datta Khel
  - **May 6, 2011:** Six civilians among those killed at a religious school

  The White House and counterterrorism official John Brennan's office declined to comment on the Bureau's findings.
