title: Drone strikes rise to one every four days
date: 2011-07-18
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The article documents the escalation of CIA drone strikes in Pakistan's tribal border regions. It details how strikes increased dramatically from one annually under President Bush to "an average of one every four days" under President Obama.

  The reporting highlights the first recorded Pakistani drone casualty in June 2004: Nek Mohammad, a Taliban leader. Significantly, the strike also killed two children—boys aged 10 and 16—representing the beginning of hundreds of civilian casualties in the campaign.

  The article notes that despite public denials from both Washington and Pakistan's government regarding drone operations, WikiLeaks cables suggest private agreements contradicted public statements. A U.S. counter-terrorism official claimed drone strikes represent "the most precise weapon in the history of warfare," yet accountability remains limited.

  The piece emphasizes persistent public anger in Pakistan, with political parties and demonstrations increasingly opposing the CIA operations. The Bureau's investigation sought disclosure of civilian casualty figures, though the CIA refused to confirm or deny the program's existence.
