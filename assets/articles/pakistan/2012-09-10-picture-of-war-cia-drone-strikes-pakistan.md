title: A Picture of War: The CIA's Drone Strikes in Pakistan
date: 2012-09-10
authors: Sarah Leo
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism has compiled an extensive database documenting reported CIA drone strikes in Pakistan since 2004. Their investigation reveals significant casualty figures from this covert campaign.

  ### Key Findings

  The Bureau's analysis indicates that "344 strikes have killed between 2,562 and 3,325" people overall. More concerning, their research documented "at least 474 civilians" killed, though they note the actual figure could reach as high as 881.

  ### Purpose

  Rather than presenting raw data, the Bureau synthesized their findings into an accessible infographic summarizing the documented facts about the drone campaign. The article emphasizes that their extensive database provides detailed information on every known attack and casualty count from the conflict.

  ### Context

  The Bureau notes this represents "a covert war that has been bitterly fought since 2004," highlighting the long duration and secretive nature of the operation.

  The article includes an infographic presenting the data visually, though the full details are available in their comprehensive drone database and detailed analysis sections.
