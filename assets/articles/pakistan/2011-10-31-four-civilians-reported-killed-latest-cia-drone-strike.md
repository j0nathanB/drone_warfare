title: Four Civilians Reported Killed in Latest CIA Drone Strike
date: 2011-10-31
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  A CIA drone strike in North Waziristan on October 30, 2011 resulted in reported civilian deaths. Initially described as killing alleged militants, subsequent reporting indicated the victims were actually workers from a local chromite mine.

  ## Key Details

  Initial reports claimed "up to six alleged militants were killed" when a vehicle was struck around noon on Sunday. However, Pakistani newspapers soon challenged this account. The Nation described the deceased as "peaceful tribesmen," while the News identified them as chromite mine workers, naming one victim as Saeedur Rahman, a chromite dealer.

  This incident marked the first reported civilian deaths in the US drone campaign since August 22, 2011.

  ## Broader Context

  The strike represented one of eight drone attacks recorded in Pakistan during October. According to the Bureau's data, the incident brought the minimum civilian death toll to 390 across the seven-year CIA campaign, with a maximum estimate of 779 deaths. Between May 2010 and this attack, 109-279 civilians were reportedly killed, including at least 14 children.

  A March 2011 strike had previously killed between 19-42 civilians during an attack on a tribal meeting concerning a chromite mine dispute.
