title: 'Drones causing mass trauma among civilians,' major study finds
date: 2012-09-25
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  A joint study by Stanford University's International Human Rights and Conflict Resolution Clinic and New York University School of Law's Global Justice Clinic examined the impact of CIA drone strikes in Pakistan's tribal areas.

  ## Key Findings

  The research, titled *Living Under Drones*, involved interviews with over 130 victims, witnesses, and experts across nine months. The study challenges the U.S. government's characterization of drone operations as precise and limited in civilian impact.

  **Psychological Effects:** Residents experience "anticipatory anxiety," insomnia, nightmares, and emotional breakdowns. Psychiatrists reported trauma levels resembling those in high-intensity conflicts.

  **Civilian Casualties:** The report documented evidence that more than 50 civilians likely died in three strikes examined in detail, contradicting U.S. official claims of minimal civilian deaths.

  **Targeting Rescuers:** The study independently corroborated previous Bureau investigations finding that secondary strikes deliberately targeted rescuers and funeral attendees. This has deterred humanitarian assistance and altered funeral practices in affected areas.

  ## Research Quality

  The report praised the Bureau's drone database as "the most reliable available source," noting its comprehensive sourcing and frequent updates compared to competing databases.

  The Obama administration had not responded to requests for engagement with the researchers as of publication.
