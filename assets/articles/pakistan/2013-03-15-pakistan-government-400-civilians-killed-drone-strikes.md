title: Pakistan Government Says 'At Least 400 Civilians' Killed in Drone Strikes
date: 2013-03-15
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  Pakistan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs has disclosed that CIA drone strikes have killed approximately 2,200 people in the country, including at least 400 civilians, with a further 200 likely to be civilian casualties. This official estimate aligns closely with the Bureau of Investigative Journalism's own research findings of 411-884 civilian deaths among 2,536-3,577 total casualties.

  ## Key Points

  The Pakistani government presented these figures to UN special rapporteur Ben Emmerson QC during his three-day visit investigating drone strike legality and ethics. Emmerson stated that "the US drone campaign in Pakistan is being conducted without consent" and constitutes a violation of Pakistan's sovereignty.

  Pakistan's representatives emphasized that the country does not consent to drone operations and views them as counterproductive, radicalizing new generations and undermining democratic processes ahead of May 2013 elections. Officials noted that terrorism has cost Pakistan $70 billion over the past decade, killing 7,000 soldiers and 40,000 civilians.

  The US has previously claimed civilian casualties typically occur "in single digits" annually. However, tribal elders from North Waziristan described civilian deaths as "commonplace," particularly among adult men engaged in ordinary activities.

  Emmerson recommended that the international community provide Pakistan's next elected government space and support for pursuing peace independently.
