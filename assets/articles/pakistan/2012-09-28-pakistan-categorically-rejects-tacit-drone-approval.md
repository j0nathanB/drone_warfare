title: Pakistan 'Categorically Rejects' Claim of Tacit Drone Strike Approval
date: 2012-09-28
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  Pakistan's Foreign Ministry firmly denied claims that it tacitly approves CIA drone operations on its territory, following revelations about the basis for U.S. assertions of permission.

  ## Key Points

  **Pakistan's Official Position:**
  The Foreign Ministry stated that "drone attacks are illegal, counterproductive, in contravention of international law and a violation of Pakistani sovereignty."

  Foreign Minister Hina Rabbani Khar acknowledged shared counterterrorism objectives but emphasized: "we have to find ways which are lawful, which are legal. The use of unilateral strikes on Pakistani territory is illegal."

  **The Alleged Legal Basis:**
  According to Wall Street Journal reporting, U.S. claims of legitimacy rest on:
  - Monthly CIA faxes to Pakistan's ISI outlining drone "flight boxes" over the tribal border region—unanswered for at least 17 months
  - Pakistan's tolerance of restricted airspace zones enabling drone operations

  **Casualty Data:**
  The Bureau documented at least 2,570 deaths from 346 U.S. drone strikes since 2004.

  **Internal U.S. Concerns:**
  Some Obama administration officials reportedly questioned the legality, with one describing the approach as "cowboy behaviour."
