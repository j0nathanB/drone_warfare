title: CIA 'Revives Attacks on Rescuers' in Pakistan
date: 2012-06-04
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  This investigative report documents CIA drone strikes in Pakistan that allegedly targeted rescue workers and funeral attendees—tactics the Bureau had previously exposed in February 2012.

  ## Key Incidents

  The article describes several strikes from late May through early June 2012:

  - **June 4:** A drone strike in Waziristan killed 16 people, with reports indicating a second attack targeted rescuers responding to the initial strike
  - **May 28:** Drones struck again near Mir Ali
  - **Late May:** A drone targeted people gathered for funeral prayers, killing up to ten individuals
  - **Late May:** A mosque strike killed at least three civilian worshippers

  ## Context

  The Bureau notes that "on more than a dozen occasions between 2009 and June 2011, the CIA attacked rescuers as they tried to retrieve the dead and injured." The last reported such attack occurred July 12, 2011, coinciding with CIA Director Leon Panetta's departure.

  The revival of these tactics corresponds with deteriorating US-Pakistan relations, particularly following Pakistan's conviction of Dr. Shakil Afridi, who aided in locating Osama bin Laden.

  ## International Response

  The UN Special Rapporteur previously called for investigation into these practices, with international lawyers questioning their legality under humanitarian law.
