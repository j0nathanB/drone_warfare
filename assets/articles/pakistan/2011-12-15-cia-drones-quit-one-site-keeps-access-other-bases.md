title: CIA Drones Quit One Pakistan Site – But US Keeps Access to Other Airbases
date: 2011-12-15
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  Despite withdrawing from Shamsi airfield, the United States maintains operational access to multiple Pakistani military bases for drone operations. Following a NATO airstrike that killed 24 Pakistani soldiers, Pakistan demanded the US leave Shamsi—a major facility in Balochistan where Predator and Reaper drones had operated for years.

  However, according to a Pakistani source with knowledge of US-Pakistan military cooperation, the US retained access to at least five other bases:

  - **Shahbaz/Jacobabad** – Original CIA drone location; armed drones may still operate here
  - **Chaklala** – Pakistan Air Force base near Islamabad handling US logistics with ~300 troops present during emergencies
  - **Quetta** – Pakistan Air Force base available for US operations "as and when" needed
  - **Tarbela** – Special Forces base used for US helicopter operations
  - **Peshawar** – Air Force base handling logistics and troop transit

  ## Key Finding

  The Bureau documented no CIA drone strikes in Pakistan since November 17, 2011, suggesting a temporary operational pause. Officials expressed concern that additional strikes could irreparably damage US-Pakistan relations, though the option remained to strike "extremely high value targets."
