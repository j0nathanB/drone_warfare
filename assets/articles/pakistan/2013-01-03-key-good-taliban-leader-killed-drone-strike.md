title: Key 'Good Taliban' Leader Killed in Pakistan Drone Strike
date: 2013-01-03
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  A CIA drone strike on January 2, 2013, killed Maulvi Nazir, a prominent militant leader based in South Waziristan, Pakistan. The strike also eliminated his two deputies, Atta Ullah and Rafey Khan, plus up to eight others.

  ### Strike Details

  Reports differed on the exact circumstances. The New York Times reported the missile struck Nazir's vehicle on the Birmal-Wana road, while the Guardian indicated he died when a house was hit during a leadership meeting. Approximately 10,000 people attended his funeral in Wana.

  A secondary strike in North Waziristan killed Pakistan Taliban commander Faisal Khan and two associates. Notably, "one missile hit a vehicle near the town, followed by another missile when people rushed to the vehicle," according to Associated Press reporting. This targeting of rescue responders has drawn controversy.

  ### Strategic Significance

  Nazir represented the so-called "Good Taliban"—militant groups maintaining truces with Pakistan's military while launching attacks across borders into Afghanistan and NATO positions. His death prompted crisis talks among Pakistani security officials over regional stability implications.

  Retired ISI brigadier Asad Munir warned of potential escalation, suggesting hostile groups might initiate "a fresh wave of terrorist activities which Pakistan may not be able to handle."

  ### Legal Developments

  On the same day, a U.S. court rejected a Freedom of Information request regarding the legal basis for drone strikes, with Judge Colleen McMahon noting an "Alice in Wonderland" situation exists regarding classified operational justifications.
