title: Infographic: The CIA's drone war in Pakistan
date: July 21, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released an interactive infographic mapping every CIA drone strike in Pakistan through the end of 2015. The visualization was created by design studio CAPTION and combines data collected by the Bureau over five years with information from the New America Foundation.

**Key Facts:**

- The first Pakistani drone strike occurred on June 17, 2004
- The Bureau documented 423 CIA or U.S. military drone strikes in Pakistan
- These strikes resulted in 2,499-4,001 total deaths, including "between 424 and 966 civilians"
- The infographic shows strike locations and casualty counts for each attack

**Context:**

The article notes the U.S. began using drones for counterterrorism in 2000 over Afghanistan. While most off-battlefield strikes have targeted Pakistan, operations also occurred in Yemen, Somalia, and Libya.

The White House released casualty data in July 2016 claiming different figures than the Bureau's independent analysis, creating discrepancies in the official record.

**About the Author:**

Jack Serle has covered drone warfare and counterterrorism operations for over five years while developing the Bureau's drone databases.
