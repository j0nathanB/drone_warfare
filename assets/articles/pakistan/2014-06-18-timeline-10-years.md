title: Interactive Timeline: 10 Years of Drone Strikes in Pakistan
date: 2014-06-18
authors: Jack Serle, Alice Ross
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism marked a significant milestone with this piece examining a decade of CIA drone operations in Pakistan. The organization's research team, which had been monitoring drone strike reports since 2011, created an interactive timeline documenting this campaign.

  **Key Findings:**

  The article reports that over 10 years, more than "380 strikes" had been conducted, resulting in approximately "2,311 or more people" killed according to the Bureau's tracking data.

  **Article Focus:**

  The piece serves as a retrospective on the campaign spanning over a decade, highlighting key milestones in the controversial operations. It was published alongside a related story featuring eyewitness accounts describing "the aftermath of the first drone strike in Pakistan."

  **Related Coverage:**

  The Bureau encouraged readers to follow authors Alice Ross and Jack Serle on Twitter for updates. The organization also promoted its monthly updates from the Covert War project and its podcast, "Drone News from the Bureau."

  **Context:**

  This article represents the Bureau's investigative journalism work in the Human Rights and Drone Warfare categories, part of their broader mission to expose injustice through data-driven research and documented reporting.
