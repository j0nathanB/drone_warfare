title: US pays €1m to Italian couple after killing their son in a drone strike
date: September 16, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The US government provided over €1 million to the family of Giovanni Lo Porto, an Italian aid worker killed in a CIA drone strike in Pakistan in January 2015. An Italian government document dated July 8, 2016, confirmed this as "a donation in the memory of Mr Giovanni Lo Porto."

The payment represents "the first confirmed example of the US paying relatives of a drone strike victim." The agreement explicitly characterized the €1.18 million as an "ex-gratia payment," meaning the US government made it voluntarily without accepting legal liability.

The January 15, 2015 attack also killed American aid worker Warren Weinstein and four al Qaeda fighters, including US citizen Ahmed Farouq. President Obama publicly acknowledged the strike in April 2015, marking the first time a US official had publicly accepted responsibility for a drone operation in Pakistan.

The agreement preserved "the US government and its officials keep their right to immunity from prosecution for the drone strike itself in an Italian court."
