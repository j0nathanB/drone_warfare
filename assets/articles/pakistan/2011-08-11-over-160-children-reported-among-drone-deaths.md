title: Over 160 Children Reported Among Drone Deaths
date: 2011-08-11
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau identified credible reports of 168 children killed across seven years of CIA drone strikes in Pakistan's tribal areas, representing approximately 44% of the minimum 385 civilian deaths documented.

  ## Major Incidents

  **2006 Madrassa Strike:** A CIA attack on a religious seminary in Chenegai, Bajaur Agency, killed up to 69 children among 80 civilians. The youngest victim was seven years old. Pakistani officials initially claimed responsibility to minimize US blame, with one aide stating: "We thought it would be less damaging if we said we did it rather than the US."

  **2010 Family Tragedy:** Din Mohammad's household suffered direct impact when a Reaper drone strike intended for militant neighbors killed his son, two daughters, and nephew—all under school age.

  ## Statistical Patterns

  During the Bush administration, 112 children were reportedly killed, with more than one-third of all strikes resulting in child fatalities. Under President Obama, 56 children were reported killed, though recent months showed significant decline.

  ## International Response

  UNICEF stated: "Even one child death from drone missiles or suicide bombings is one child death too many."

  Pakistani lawyer Mirza Shahzad Akbar warned that child casualties serve as "recruitment agents for militants in the area."
