title: Fresh evidence of CIA civilian deaths in Pakistan revealed
date: 2012-02-27
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports on two major investigations documenting civilian casualties from CIA drone strikes in Pakistan's tribal areas.

  ### Associated Press Investigation

  The Associated Press deployed field researchers to Waziristan, interviewing over 80 local residents about ten CIA strikes since 2010. Of 194 people killed, AP confirmed 138 were militants, with the remaining 56 being civilians or tribal police.

  The investigation identified previously unreported civilian deaths:

  - **August 14, 2010:** Seven civilians, including a ten-year-old child, died during Ramadan prayers alongside seven Taliban members, contrary to U.S. official assessments.

  - **April 22, 2011:** Three children and two women were killed in an attack on a guest house in Spinwan, confirmed by three named eyewitnesses.

  - **May 6, 2011:** AP independently confirmed six civilians died alongside ten Taliban members in a restaurant strike, validating earlier Bureau findings that had been dismissed by unnamed U.S. officials.

  ### Reprieve's UN Complaint

  The legal charity filed a case with the UN Human Rights Council supported by affidavits from 18 family members of civilians killed in CIA attacks, many children.

  One case detailed: "a missile struck nearby, killing Maezol's eight-year-old son" on February 14, 2009, with approximately 30 additional casualties.

  A June 15, 2011 strike killed four civilians—a pharmacist, his assistant, a student, and a water authority employee—provoking spontaneous highway protests at their funeral.

  Reprieve director Clive Stafford Smith stated the CIA was "creating desolation" and urged UN intervention before additional children die.
