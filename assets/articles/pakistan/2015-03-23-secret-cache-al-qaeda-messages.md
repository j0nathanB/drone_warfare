title: Secret Cache of Al Qaeda Messages Corroborates Bureau Drone Strike Reports in Pakistan
date: 2015-03-23
authors: Owen Bennett-Jones
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism obtained private messages between Osama bin Laden and his manager Atiyah Abd al Rahman, discovered during the 2011 Navy SEAL raid on bin Laden's Abbottabad compound. These documents, released as evidence in a US terrorism trial, validate many details in the Bureau's drone strike reporting.

  ## Key Findings

  **Three strikes corroborated:**

  The documents detail three specific 2010 drone operations already documented in the Bureau's database:

  1. **Strike Ob94 (May 21, 2010):** Killed al Qaeda finance chief Mustafa abu al Yazid along with family members. According to Mahmud's account, Yazid's son arrived shortly before the strike to relocate his father but found him sleeping and left him undisturbed—a decision that proved fatal.

  2. **Strike Ob161 (November 16, 2010):** Killed 20 Abu Bakr al Sidiq Brigade members gathered for Eid celebrations despite orders limiting gatherings to five people.

  3. **Strike Ob101 (June 29, 2010):** Killed senior leader Hamzah al Jufi.

  ## Civilian Casualty Details

  The Yazid strike killed his Egyptian wife Umm al Shaymah, three daughters, and granddaughter Hasfah. One daughter survived with injuries. The Bureau states: "The names of Umm al Shaymah and Hasfah have been added to the list of drone victims being compiled by the Bureau's Naming The Dead project."

  ## Al Qaeda's Surveillance Concerns

  Mahmud expressed frustration about espionage networks, noting: "Our brothers and other Pashtun security units are constantly uncovering and destroying spies' networks...That has not kept airstrikes from hitting us repeatedly because we continue to make mistakes."

  The documents reveal Al Qaeda's extreme counter-surveillance measures, including instructions to avoid using vehicles, cease operations to reduce movement, and bin Laden's concern that one wife's Iranian dental filling might contain a tracking device.

  ## Methodological Validation

  The newly released documents validate the Bureau's open-source research methodology, which critics had questioned. The materials confirm the Bureau accurately identified major targets and refined casualty figures when conflicting accounts existed.
