title: Naming the dead: Only 10 of scores killed by US drones in Pakistan last year have been identified
date: February 3, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that of the dozens killed by US drone strikes in Pakistan during 2015, only 10 victims were identified. According to the findings, "The names for all 10 came from either terrorist propaganda or the US government."

Pakistani officials notably declined to assist in identification efforts for the first time since drone operations commenced in 2004. This contrasts sharply with previous cooperation patterns.

The broader context reveals significant accountability gaps: across all drone strikes since 2004, approximately 2,494 people were killed, yet only 729 have been named. "At least 1,765 victims remain nameless," highlighting the scale of unidentified casualties.

Of the 2015 victims identified, two were aid workers killed inadvertently (Giovanni Lo Porto and Warren Weinstein), five were associated with al Qaeda, and three belonged to the Pakistan Taliban. Four names surfaced through US government investigations, while six emerged from terrorist organization propaganda materials.

The article notes that identification sources traditionally include intelligence officials, government representatives, and armed group communications, though 2015 represented a departure from this pattern.
