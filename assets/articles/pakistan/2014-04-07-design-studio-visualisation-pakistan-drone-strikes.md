title: Design studio Pitch Interactive's visualisation of Pakistan drone strikes uses Bureau data
date: 2014-04-07
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  Pitch Interactive, a California-based design studio, created an interactive timeline documenting CIA drone operations in Pakistan's tribal areas, spanning from June 17, 2004 through December 25, 2013. The visualization incorporates data compiled by the Bureau of Investigative Journalism.

  The project highlights periods of concentrated CIA activity. Notably, the article reports that "more than 100 days have passed since the CIA last launched a drone strike in Pakistan," marking the longest documented pause in the campaign to that date. This extended break substantially exceeded the previous record—a 54-day hiatus ending in 2011.

  The timing coincided with peace negotiations between Pakistan's government and the Pakistan Taliban. According to journalist Rahimullah Yusufzai, who participated in talks with the TTP, "Islamabad had asked the US for a cessation of drone strikes during the peace talks."

  The strikes had been targeting Pakistan's tribal territories for approximately a decade by the article's publication.
