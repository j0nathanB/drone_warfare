title: US Officials Attack 'Far From Authoritative' Leaked Drone Report
date: 2013-07-25
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism obtained a secret Pakistani government document detailing over 70 CIA drone strikes between 2006-2009. The report indicated that at least 147 of 746 people killed were civilians, with potential civilian deaths reaching 220.

  ## Key Developments

  **US Official Response:**
  Unnamed American officials disputed the leaked report's credibility, claiming it derived from "indirect input from a loose network of Pakistani government contacts." One official stated the findings were "far from authoritative" and dismissed claims of civilian casualties as "ludicrous."

  **Pakistani Government Position:**
  Earlier that year, Pakistan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs informed UN special rapporteur Ben Emmerson that CIA drones had killed approximately 2,200 people, including at least 400 civilians, with another 200 likely civilians.

  **Expert Critique:**
  Shahzad Akbar of Reprieve legal charity questioned how the US could accurately assess civilian deaths when targeting information was frequently inaccurate. He noted that "drone surveillance alone cannot determine who is militant and who is not."

  **Historical Context:**
  Retired Brigadier Mahmood Shah revealed that early CIA intelligence was highly unreliable—of 28 identified militant locations, 27 were incorrect.

  **Bureau's Assessment:**
  The organization estimated 410-928 civilian casualties among 2,509-3,576 total deaths from Pakistani drone strikes since 2004.
