title: Consensus grows among UN states for greater transparency on drone civilian deaths
date: 2013-10-26
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  Key UN member states, including US allies like the UK and EU, broke with precedent by endorsing calls for increased transparency regarding civilian casualties from drone strikes. This represented a significant shift from a decade of resistance to such demands.

  ## Key Points

  **International Support for Transparency:**
  Several nations joined forces to advocate for openness. Pakistan was notably vocal, stating there was "no implicit or explicit consent" to US drone strikes on its territory.

  **UN Investigation Details:**
  Special rapporteurs Ben Emmerson and Christof Heyns presented reports examining legal and ethical issues surrounding armed drones, particularly in non-traditional conflicts. Heyns emphasized: "The drone should follow the law, not the law follow the drone."

  **Specific Demands:**
  Heyns' report called for states to "publicly disclose the legal basis for drone use, operational responsibility, criteria for targeting, impact including civilian casualties."

  **Documented Concerns:**
  The reports highlighted evidence of "double-tap" strikes—deliberately targeting first responders at initial attack sites. Organizations including Amnesty International and the Bureau have documented this practice.

  **Next Steps:**
  Emmerson's team was examining 33 problematic strikes by the US, Israel, and UK, with a final report expected in 2014.
