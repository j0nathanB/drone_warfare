title: US Claims of 'No Civilian Deaths' Are Untrue
date: 2011-07-18
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism's investigation directly challenges assertions by President Obama's counterterrorism adviser John Brennan that there had been zero civilian casualties from CIA drone strikes in Pakistan since August 2010.

  ## Key Findings

  The Bureau examined 116 reported CIA drone strikes in Pakistan between August 23, 2010 and June 29, 2011. The investigation identified:

  - **45-56 civilian deaths** across 10 confirmed strikes
  - **6 identified children** among those killed
  - **15 additional strikes** requiring urgent investigation with potentially 66+ more civilian deaths
  - **740+ total deaths** from all 116 strikes examined

  ## Central Claims Disputed

  John Brennan stated: "nearly for the past year there hasn't been a single collateral death" and that operations were "exceptionally precise."

  However, the Bureau documented civilian casualties in more than one in five strikes examined, contradicting official U.S. government denials.

  ## Official Response

  A senior U.S. official rejected the Bureau's findings as "wildly inaccurate" while refusing to provide contradicting evidence. Both the White House and Brennan's office declined to comment on the investigation's findings.

  Human rights lawyer Clive Stafford Smith expressed concern that "U.S. intelligence services" might be misleading the President about actual casualty figures.
