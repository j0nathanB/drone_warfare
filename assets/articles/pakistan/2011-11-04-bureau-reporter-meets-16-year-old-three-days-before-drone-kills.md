title: Bureau Reporter Meets 16-Year-Old Three Days Before US Drone Kills Him
date: 2011-11-04
authors: Pratap Chatterjee
content: |
  Bureau investigative journalist Pratap Chatterjee documented a tragic case of civilian casualties from US drone strikes in Pakistan. He met Tariq Aziz, a 16-year-old from Waziristan, at a grand conference in Islamabad on October 27-28, 2011, where the youth was participating in a project to document drone strike aftermath.

  Just 72 hours after attending the gathering—which included politician Imran Khan and legal charity representatives—Tariq and his 12-year-old cousin Waheed Khan were killed when two missiles struck their vehicle as they traveled near Norak.

  ## Key Details

  The boys' deaths brought the total child casualties from documented drone strikes to 175. The Bureau had recorded 306 strikes that killed between 2,359 and 2,959 people, with over 85% conducted under President Obama's administration.

  Tariq came from a poor family; his father worked abroad in the UAE. Waheed earned approximately £15 monthly at a local shop. Both lived in communities near the Afghanistan border.

  Legal advocate Shahzad Akbar questioned the targeting: "We wanted to work with youth, to include them in accountability efforts," highlighting the lack of due process against the teenagers.
