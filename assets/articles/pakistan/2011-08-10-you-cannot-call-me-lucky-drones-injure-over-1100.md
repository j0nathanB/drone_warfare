title: 'You cannot call me lucky' – drones injure over 1,100
date: 2011-08-10
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism documents the injuries sustained by over 1,100 people affected by CIA drone operations in Pakistan's tribal areas.

  **Key Subject:**
  The article centers on 15-year-old Sadaullah, a student who lost both legs and an eye when US missiles struck his family home in Machi Khel, North Waziristan, on September 7, 2009. The attack targeted a militant leader who wasn't present. His uncle and two cousins were killed.

  **Legal Perspective:**
  According to lawyer Mirza Shahzad Akbar, representing Sadaullah: "The injured who survive with their severed limbs, they often tell me, 'you cannot really call me lucky'" because "There are no facilities for the disabled in Waziristan, such people can have zero opportunities ahead of them in life."

  **Scope of Injuries:**
  The Bureau identified at least 1,117 documented injury cases from drone strikes, affecting both militants and civilians, including children. Notable incidents include:
  - Up to 50 people injured in an early 2009 strike on an alleged Taliban training camp
  - 41 people injured in a separate Kurram Agency strike

  **Health Concerns:**
  Local doctors report increased use of tranquilizers and sleeping pills among residents, suggesting psychological impacts beyond physical injuries.
