title: CIA Tactics in Pakistan Include Targeting Rescuers and Funerals
date: 2012-02-04
authors: Chris Woods, Christina Lamb
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism revealed that CIA drone operations in Pakistan have killed dozens of civilians attempting rescue efforts or attending funerals. The investigation contradicted President Obama's claim that drone strikes were "targeted" operations causing minimal civilian casualties.

  ## Key Findings

  **Civilian Casualties:**
  - Between 282 and 535 civilians credibly reported killed since Obama took office
  - More than 60 children among the deceased
  - At least 50 civilians killed in follow-up strikes while assisting victims
  - Over 20 civilians killed in deliberate strikes on funerals and mourners

  **Scope of Operations:**
  260 drone attacks conducted by the Obama administration in Pakistan—averaging one every four days.

  ## Notable Incidents

  **Khaisoor Attack (May 16, 2009):**
  Initial strike killed Taliban militants; follow-up missile strikes targeted rescuers attempting recovery operations, resulting in approximately 29 deaths including six villagers.

  **Funeral Strike (June 23, 2009):**
  CIA deliberately targeted a funeral of militant commander Khwaz Wali Mehsud to "shake loose" Taliban leader Baitullah Mehsud. The strike killed up to 83 people, with approximately 45 civilians including ten children.

  ## Legal Concerns

  International law experts argue these operations constitute "state-sanctioned extra-judicial executions." Naz Modirzadeh, Harvard's Program on Humanitarian Policy and Conflict Research, stated: "If it is not in a situation of armed conflict...then it is an extra-judicial execution."

  UN Special Rapporteur Christof Heyns called repeat strikes on medical personnel "very worrying" and potentially "crimes of war."

  ## Strategic Questions

  Military officials raised concerns about effectiveness. Brigadier Abdullah Dogar questioned whether killing mostly innocent civilians created "strategic advantage," noting such strikes generate anti-American sentiment in a nuclear-armed nation.
