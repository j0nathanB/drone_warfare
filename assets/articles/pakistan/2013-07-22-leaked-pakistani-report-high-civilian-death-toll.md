title: Leaked Pakistani Report Confirms High Civilian Death Toll in CIA Drone Strikes
date: 2013-07-22
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism obtained a classified Pakistani government document detailing drone strike casualties in the Federally Administered Tribal Areas (FATA) between 2006 and 2009. The findings substantially contradict U.S. claims about civilian deaths.

  ## Key Findings

  **Casualty Discrepancies:**
  - The leaked document lists 746 people killed across 75 CIA drone strikes
  - At least 147 are identified as civilians, including 94 children
  - U.S. officials claimed only 50-60 non-combatants died throughout the entire nine-year campaign

  **Notable Case:**
  A 2006 strike on a religious school in Bajaur Agency resulted in 81 reported civilian deaths, predominantly children, despite Pakistani military denials. Former President Musharraf later claimed those killed were "militants doing training."

  ## Document Credibility

  Former Political Agent Rauf Khan Khattak stated the data came from ground sources and was "reasonably accurate" because officials had "no benefit in cooking the books" for an internal document never intended for public release.

  ## Discrepancies

  The document shows fewer reported civilian deaths after Obama's 2009 election, though independent research documented civilian casualties in multiple 2009 strikes that the Pakistani report omitted.
