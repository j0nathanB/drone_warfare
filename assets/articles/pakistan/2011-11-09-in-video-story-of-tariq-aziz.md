title: In Video: The story of Tariq Aziz
date: 2011-11-09
authors: Pratap Chatterjee
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism documented the case of Tariq Aziz, a 16-year-old killed in a CIA drone strike in Pakistan on October 31, 2011.

  Days before his death, Tariq attended a significant gathering in Islamabad organized to protest CIA operations in Pakistan's tribal regions. The event brought together political figures including former cricketer Imran Khan, human rights lawyer Clive Stafford Smith, and other activists and officials.

  Tariq had traveled to the capital specifically to participate in this "Jirga" (traditional council meeting) and learn photography skills. His goal was to document events unfolding in his home region of North Waziristan. According to his uncle, Tariq was motivated to act after losing a cousin in a previous strike.

  The conference featured photographer Noor Behram, who documents drone strike effects on Pakistani civilians. Tariq aspired to follow a similar path as a documentarian. However, he was killed just days after hearing Behram speak.

  The article notes this case as part of "the Bureau's study on the Covert Drone War," an ongoing investigation tracking civilian casualties from US drone operations.
