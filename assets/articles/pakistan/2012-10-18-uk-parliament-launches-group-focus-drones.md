title: UK Parliament Launches Group to Focus on Drones
date: 2012-10-18
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  The All-Party Parliamentary Group on Drones was established to examine the expanding use of unmanned aerial vehicles in military and civilian contexts. Labour MP Tom Watson serves as president, with Conservative Zac Goldsmith as vice president.

  ## Key Points

  **Legal and Ethical Concerns:**
  Clive Stafford Smith from Reprieve characterized U.S. drone operations as constituting "death penalty without trial," highlighting serious questions about the legal framework and secrecy surrounding these campaigns.

  **Global Proliferation:**
  Research presented indicated that 76 countries currently possess some form of unmanned aerial vehicle, from Botswana to Lithuania, extending far beyond major military powers.

  **UK Operations:**
  The United Kingdom operates five drone models, with only the Reaper armed. Since 2008, British forces conducted 319 strikes in Afghanistan using aircraft piloted from Nevada's Creech Air Force Base. The government had also conducted drone missions in Libya, despite earlier denying such operations.

  **Civilian Implications:**
  Current regulations permit recreational drone operation within 150 feet of people and property, raising privacy concerns. Neither aviation authorities nor industry-led guidelines adequately address these implications, according to experts consulted.
