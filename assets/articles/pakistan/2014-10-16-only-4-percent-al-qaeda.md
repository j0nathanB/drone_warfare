title: Only 4% of Drone Victims in Pakistan Named as al Qaeda Members
date: 2014-10-16
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  As the U.S. conducted its 400th drone strike in Pakistan, the Bureau of Investigative Journalism released findings from its "Naming the Dead" project that challenged official justifications for the operation.

  ## Key Findings

  The investigation identified 2,379 people killed in CIA drone strikes in Pakistan since June 2004. Of these:

  - Only 704 have been named and identified
  - Just 295 were reported as members of armed groups
  - A mere 84 were identified as al Qaeda members (less than 4% of total deaths)
  - Only 111 were described as senior commanders (5% of total)

  ## Official Response

  When asked to comment, U.S. National Security Council spokeswoman Caitlin Hayden stated that strikes occur only with "near-certainty" that civilians won't be harmed, adding: "The death of innocent civilians is something that the U.S. Government seeks to avoid if at all possible."

  ## Analysis and Context

  Mustafa Qadri from Amnesty International observed: "Judging by the sheer volume of strikes and reliable estimates of total casualties, it is very unlikely that the majority of victims are senior commanders."

  The research contradicted Secretary of State John Kerry's earlier claim that only "confirmed terrorist targets at the highest level" were targeted.

  The findings highlighted the ambiguity surrounding "associated forces" of al Qaeda and the Taliban, and revealed limited transparency in U.S. drone operations.
