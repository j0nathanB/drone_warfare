title: Judges greet US government drone secrecy with skepticism
date: 2012-09-21
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  Three federal appeals court judges expressed skepticism regarding the Obama administration's attempt to withhold information about the CIA's targeted killing program during a hearing on Thursday.

  The American Civil Liberties Union challenged the government to release records detailing the legal justification and policy decisions underlying the CIA's drone operations against alleged militants in foreign nations. However, the administration refused to even acknowledge whether such records exist.

  Judge Merrick Garland highlighted the government's contradictory position, noting that officials were asking the court to deny what had already been publicly discussed. He referenced John Brennan's speech on counterterrorism strategy as evidence of official acknowledgment of the drone program.

  Jameel Jaffer, ACLU deputy director, observed that "all three judges questioned the government aggressively about the disconnect between its position in court…and the many statements it has made publicly about the programme."

  ## Key Details

  The hearing represented the latest development in a two-and-a-half-year legal dispute. The ACLU also sought information regarding:

  - The killing of Anwar al-Awlaki in a Yemen drone strike
  - A 2009 cruise missile strike in Yemen that killed 22 children

  The Bureau filed an amicus brief supporting the ACLU's argument that the CIA drone program "is so widely acknowledged and heavily reported upon that it can hardly be called a secret anymore."
