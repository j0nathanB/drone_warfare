title: Is Pakistan drone strike hiatus linked to peace talks?
date: 2014-02-06
authors: Patrick Galey
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism found that US drone strikes disrupted Pakistan-Taliban peace negotiations at least four times during 2013. As of the article's publication, a 43-day hiatus marked the longest break in strikes over two years, coinciding with resumed peace talks.

  ## Key Findings

  **Pattern of Disrupted Negotiations:**
  - January 2, 2013: Maulvi Nazir, leader of a militant faction cooperating with Pakistan, was killed by drone strike one day after peace negotiations began
  - May 29, 2013: Deputy commander Wali Ur Rehman was killed, prompting the Taliban to rescind their peace offer
  - November 1, 2013: TTP commander Hakimullah Mehsud was killed the same night high-level negotiations were announced
  - November 21, 2013: Haqqani Network member Ahmad Jan was killed despite US assurances of restraint

  **Official Positions:**

  Pakistani negotiator Rahimullah Yusufzai stated: "Pakistani officials have discussed this with the US ambassador and said that serious talks would be derailed by a drone strike."

  However, a White House official denied any formal agreement, calling Taliban negotiations "entirely an internal matter for Pakistan."

  **Analyst Assessment:**

  Abdullah Khan from the Conflict Monitoring Centre noted the current lull demonstrated US flexibility, but warned high-value targets could still disrupt future talks.
