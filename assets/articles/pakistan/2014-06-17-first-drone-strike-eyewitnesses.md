title: Ten Years On: Eyewitnesses Describe the Aftermath of the First Pakistan Drone Strike
date: 2014-06-17
authors: Alice Ross
content: |
  On June 17, 2004, the CIA conducted its first drone strike in Pakistan, targeting Taliban leader Nek Mohammed in South Waziristan. While Mohammed was killed, the strike also resulted in civilian deaths, including two children.

  **Key Details:**

  Mohammed, a senior militant commander in his late twenties, had organized tribal resistance against the Pakistani government. Following failed military efforts to eliminate him, Pakistani intelligence reportedly offered the US permission to conduct drone operations if they could eliminate Mohammed.

  An eyewitness journalist described arriving at the scene after the strike: "At the time people never knew that buzzing was a drone. Later, we became used to it. A flash of light came. There was a big blast."

  The journalist identified the deceased children as Mohammed Zaman and Khan Zaman, sons of homeowner Sher Zaman, along with local commander Shahrukh Khan. However, a medical worker present at a nearby dispensary recalled only Mohammed being brought in wounded, creating conflicting accounts.

  An Amnesty International researcher noted the difficulty in confirmation: "It's extremely challenging, and it's possible that these children are being identified by different names by different sources."

  Despite the documented civilian casualties, no accountability followed a decade later.
