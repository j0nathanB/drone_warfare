title: Sorry for killing your son, CIA tells drone strike victim's family
date: October 28, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

US officials held a private meeting with the family of Giovanni Lo Porto, an Italian aid worker killed in a CIA drone strike in Pakistan in January 2015. This briefing marked an unprecedented transparency moment in America's covert drone operations.

**Key Details:**

- Lo Porto, 37, and fellow aid worker Warren Weinstein, 73, were killed alongside three al Qaeda operatives in the strike
- US officials stated that "all procedures were followed" and intelligence indicated no hostages were present
- The family received a €1.2 million "donation in memory" from the US government
- Officials claimed independent "Red Team" analysts reviewed the operation before and after, finding no procedural violations
- Evidence suggests the aid workers were held underground, potentially explaining why surveillance didn't detect them
- The family's lawyer noted the briefing failed to satisfy their demand for transparency: "It is difficult to believe that Giovanni and Weinstein were held in the underground for 24 hours a day for a month"

**Significance:** This represents the first known instance of US officials meeting with a drone strike victim's family to provide an explanation and apology during the drone war era.
