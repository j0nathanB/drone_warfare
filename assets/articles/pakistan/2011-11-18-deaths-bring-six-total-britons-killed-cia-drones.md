title: Deaths bring to six total Britons killed by CIA drones
date: 2011-11-18
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported that two British nationals, Ibrahim Adam and Mohammed Azmir, were believed killed in a US drone strike in Pakistan's Waziristan region. According to family accounts shared with the Press Association, the deaths occurred at least three months prior to the article's publication.

  This incident brought the documented total of British nationals killed in CIA strikes since 2008 to at least six individuals. Notably, Azmir's brother, Abdul Jabbar, had been killed in a previous drone strike on October 4, 2010.

  ## Documented British Casualties in CIA Strikes

  The article identified the following casualties:

  - **Rashid Rauf** (November 22, 2008) – Al Qaeda operative suspected in a 2006 plane-bombing plot
  - **Abdul Jabbar** (October 4, 2010) – killed alongside nine others
  - **Mr Stephen/Abu Bakr** and **Mr Dearsmith/Mansoor Ahmed** (December 10, 2010) – Muslim converts killed in a car attack
  - **Ibrahim Adam** and **Mohammed Azmir** (before September 2011) – killed in Waziristan

  Ibrahim Adam's father reportedly confirmed his 24-year-old son's death. Azmir, aged 37, had been subject to UK asset-freezing orders since February 2010.
