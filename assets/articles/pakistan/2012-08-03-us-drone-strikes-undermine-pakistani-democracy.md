title: 'US drone strikes undermine Pakistani democracy' says top diplomat
date: 2012-08-03
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  Pakistan's High Commissioner to the UK, Wajid Shamsul Hasan, warned that CIA drone operations in Pakistan's tribal areas are damaging democratic institutions and pushing citizens toward extremist movements.

  ## Key Points

  **Democratic Impact:**
  The ambassador stated that drone strikes undermine governance: "when you pass a resolution against drone attacks in the parliament, and nothing happens. The Americans don't listen to you."

  **Public Sentiment:**
  Hasan emphasized growing anti-American sentiment, noting that despite significant US financial aid, "they're the most hated country in Pakistan among the people" due to drone operations.

  **Sovereignty Concerns:**
  He characterized the strikes as violations, asserting: "This is a violation of the UN Charter, it is a clear violation of our territorial sovereignty."

  **Casualty Figures:**
  The High Commissioner referenced 2,500-3,000 deaths from drone attacks, with estimates of at least 482 civilian casualties, though he suggested official US claims underestimated civilian harm.

  **US-Pakistan Relations:**
  Hasan criticized American foreign policy inconsistency, suggesting the US historically preferred dealing with individual leaders rather than democratic institutions in the region.

  **Afghanistan Strategy:**
  He questioned NATO's decade-long presence without eliminating Taliban or al-Qaeda operations, advocating instead for Pakistan-led dialogue initiatives.
