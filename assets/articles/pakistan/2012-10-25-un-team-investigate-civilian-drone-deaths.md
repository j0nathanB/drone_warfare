title: UN Team to Investigate Civilian Drone Deaths
date: 2012-10-25
authors: The Bureau
content: |
  The United Nations announced plans to establish a Geneva-based investigation unit to examine civilian casualties from US covert drone strikes. UN special rapporteur Ben Emmerson QC made the announcement at Harvard Law School on October 25, 2012.

  ## Key Points

  **Investigation Scope**
  The unit will investigate individual drone attacks and examine "other forms of targeted killing conducted in counter-terrorism operations" where civilians allegedly died. It will seek explanations from states using and hosting this technology, beginning work in early 2013.

  **War Crimes Designation**
  Emmerson endorsed labeling deliberate strikes on rescuers and funeral attendees as war crimes. According to the Bureau's previous reporting, at least 50 civilians were killed in follow-up rescue strikes under Obama, and over 20 in funeral attacks.

  **Political Context**
  Emmerson criticized both President Obama and Republican challenger Mitt Romney for pursuing drone programs without public debate. He noted that Russia, China, and Pakistan had called for investigations during UN Human Rights Council sessions in June 2012.

  **Background**
  UN investigators had previously expressed concerns about US "extrajudicial executions" since 2002. The Bureau initially exposed the practice of targeting rescuers and funeral attendees in February 2012.
