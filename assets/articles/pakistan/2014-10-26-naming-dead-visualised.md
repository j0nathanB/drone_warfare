title: Naming the Dead: visualised
date: 2014-10-26
authors: Vic Parsons
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released an interactive visualization of data from its "Naming the Dead" project, which tracks individuals killed in CIA drone strikes in Pakistan. Created by developer Basile Simon, the visualization illustrates the limited knowledge about victims' identities.

  **Key Findings:**

  As of the publication date, researchers had identified 704 of approximately 2,379 people killed by U.S. drone strikes in Pakistan since 2011. Among the named individuals:

  - 322 were reportedly civilians, including 99 children
  - 295 were alleged militants
  - 87 remained classified as unknown

  The database included only two women: Bibi Mamana, a Pakistani grandmother listed as a civilian, and Raquel Burgos Garcia, a 31-year-old Spanish citizen whose classification shifted from civilian to unknown due to potential al Qaeda connections.

  **Methodology:**

  The research drew from multiple sources including leaked Pakistani government records, open-source reports in English, Pashtun, and Urdu, plus field investigations and data from organizations like Amnesty International and Reprieve.

  **Notable Conclusion:**

  A concurrent investigation found that merely "4% of drone victims in Pakistan have been named as al Qaeda members," highlighting the challenge of identifying actual militant targets versus civilian casualties.
