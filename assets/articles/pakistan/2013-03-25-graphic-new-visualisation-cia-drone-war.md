title: Graphic New Visualisation for CIA's Drone War in Pakistan
date: 2013-03-25
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism partnered with Pitch Interactive to create an interactive visualization documenting CIA drone strikes in Pakistan from 2004 onwards. The project, titled "Out of Sight, Out of Mind," integrates casualty data from the Bureau's Covert Drone War project and the New America Foundation.

  ## Key Points

  The visualization displays every known drone strike and casualty through an interactive timeline showing the campaign's progression and human toll. Wesley Grubbs of Pitch Interactive stated the team aimed to help people "see this in that light" they hadn't considered before, as they believed "drone strikes are a very hot topic right now but we feel people are being misled."

  The project emerged amid intensified public debate following CIA director John Brennan's Senate confirmation hearing. UN special rapporteur Ben Emmerson had recently reported that CIA drones killed approximately 2,200 people in Pakistan, including at least 400 civilians based on Pakistani authorities' counts.

  The Bureau simultaneously launched a related initiative called "Naming the Dead," encouraging readers to support identification efforts for drone strike victims through the Freedom of the Press Foundation.
