title: Deadliest Drone Strike in Nine Months Bucks Recent Trend
date: 2013-07-05
authors: Alice Ross, Jack Serle
content: |
  A drone strike in Miranshah, North Waziristan, Pakistan on July 3, 2013, resulted in 16-18 deaths, marking the deadliest attack in nine months. Multiple drones simultaneously fired four missiles at a vehicle and building, with one local resident describing the impact: "They simultaneously fired four huge missiles and jolted the entire town."

  The strike contradicted a recent trend toward fewer casualties per attack. The Bureau's data showed an average of 3.9 deaths per strike in early 2013, down significantly from 11.4 in the same 2010 period.

  **Key findings:**
  - Only two other strikes in the previous two years had killed 16+ people
  - About 1 in 13 strikes historically achieved such high body counts
  - The target site was reportedly a Haqqani Network stronghold
  - Pakistan's Foreign Ministry condemned the strike as violating national sovereignty

  Experts suggested the decline in casualties reflected either policy changes regarding controversial "signature strikes" or behavioral shifts among militants who now avoid gathering in large numbers in exposed locations.
