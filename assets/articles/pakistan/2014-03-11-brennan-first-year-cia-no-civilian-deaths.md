title: John Brennan Marks First Year at CIA Amid No Confirmed Civilian Drone Deaths in Pakistan
date: 2014-03-11
authors: Jack Serle
content: |
  During John Brennan's first year as CIA director (appointed January 2013), no confirmed civilian casualties resulted from drone strikes in Pakistan, according to Bureau analysis. The frequency and casualty rates of drone operations in Pakistan have declined since 2010, with over two months elapsing without strikes at the time of publication.

  ## Key Points

  **Brennan's Background:**
  Brennan served as President Obama's chief counter-terrorism adviser from January 2009 before becoming CIA director. He was instrumental in shaping targeted killing policies beyond traditional battlefields. He famously stated: "There are things the agency has been involved in since 9/11 that...have been a bit of an aberration from the traditional role."

  **Declining Trends:**
  - No confirmed civilian deaths under Brennan's leadership
  - Strikes decreased significantly from their peak
  - A 75-day hiatus preceded the article's publication, coinciding with Pakistan peace talks

  **Historical Context:**
  Previous directors oversaw bloodier campaigns. Leon Panetta (2009-2011) conducted 223 strikes killing at least 1,482 people, including 212+ civilians. Michael Hayden (2006-2009) averaged over 8 deaths per strike and 3+ civilian deaths per strike.

  **Yemen Operations:**
  The agency shifted focus geographically; Yemen saw increased civilian casualties per strike compared to previous years.
