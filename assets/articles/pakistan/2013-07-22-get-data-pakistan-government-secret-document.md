title: Get the Data: The Pakistan government's secret document
date: 2013-07-22
authors: Chris Woods
content: |
  The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published a leaked internal Pakistani government document titled "Details of Attacks by NATO Forces/Predators in FATA." This report contains Pakistan's own estimates of casualties from CIA drone strikes.

  The document covers January 13, 2006 through October 24, 2009, compiled from field reports by local officials in Pakistan's Federally Administered Tribal Areas. It documents over 70 drone strikes during this period, plus additional incidents involving NATO and unspecified forces.

  **Key findings from the leaked report:**

  - 746 people listed as killed in drone strikes
  - At least 147 identified as civilian victims
  - Approximately 94 of the deceased were children

  The Bureau noted significant gaps in the documentation, including missing records for all five reported strikes in 2007 and absent biographical details about victims. While victim genders and child casualties were sometimes recorded, names and identifying information were generally omitted. The report also lacked information about several senior militant commanders known to have died in these attacks.

  The Bureau published the document in full, removing only minor distinguishing marks, citing strong public interest value. A follow-up report covering strikes through September 2013 was obtained and published in January 2014.
