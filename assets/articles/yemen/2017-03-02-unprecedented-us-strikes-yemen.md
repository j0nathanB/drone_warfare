title: Unprecedented number of US drone and air strikes hit Yemen
date: March 2, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

The United States conducted an unprecedented intensification of counter-terrorism operations in Yemen by carrying out 20 strikes across three provinces in a single morning. The Department of Defense confirmed the strikes targeted fighters from AQAP (al Qaeda's regional affiliate), their equipment, and infrastructure in Abyan, al Bayda, and Shabwah provinces.

According to Pentagon Spokesman Captain Jeff Davis, the operations were coordinated with Yemen's President Hadi. However, the Pentagon statement omitted casualty figures, though Yemeni officials reported seven deaths from two strikes.

The timing followed a controversial January 29 special forces raid on Yakla village that killed 25 civilians, including nine children under 13, plus one Navy SEAL. A Pentagon official later confirmed these casualty numbers.

**Context on strike frequency:** The 20 strikes represent a dramatic spike. The US averaged three strikes monthly in 2016 and never exceeded two in a single day. During 2012's Abyan offensive against AQAP, the maximum recorded daily strikes was four.

**Historical data:** The Bureau documented at least 186 US air, drone strikes, and special forces raids since 2002, resulting in approximately 853 deaths—158 reportedly civilians.
