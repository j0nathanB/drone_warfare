title: The Civilian Massacre the US Neither Confirms nor Denies
date: March 29, 2012
authors: Chris Woods
content:

This investigative article documents a December 17, 2009 US cruise missile strike on al-Majala, Yemen that killed 44 civilians alongside its intended militant target.

## Key Details

**The Attack:**
A US Navy vessel launched at least one BGM-109D Tomahawk cruise missile at the hamlet of al-Majala. The intended target was Saleh Mohammed al-Anbouri, a known al Qaeda-linked militant. However, the strike also killed civilians—entire families with no connection to terrorism.

**The Casualties:**
- 44 civilians died in the initial strike
- 22 children killed, including one-year-old Khadje Ali Mokbel Louqye
- 12 women dead, five reportedly pregnant
- 3 additional deaths from cluster munitions left behind

**Official Response:**
Yemen's parliamentary commission investigated and published findings in Arabic on February 7, 2010, documenting all victims' names and ages. Yemen later paid local compensation to families, but "the American authorities did not get involved in this process in any way."

**US Accountability:**
Despite possessing detailed knowledge of the casualties, the Pentagon and State Department declined to confirm investigations or disciplinary measures. General David Petraeus initially claimed only "the wife and two children" of al-Anbouri died—figures contradicted by documented evidence.
