title: First civilian death in a drone strike this year reported in Yemen
date: January 17, 2014
authors: Jack Serle
content:

A Yemeni farmer became the first reported civilian casualty from a drone strike in 2014, killed in a suspected US strike in Yemen. Witnesses described the victim as a farmer struck by shrapnel, with some reports indicating he was "blown into pieces by the force of the blast."

## Key Details

The strike targeted a vehicle allegedly carrying three al Qaeda members, with drones firing three missiles at the target. While unnamed officials claimed an al Qaeda member was killed, witnesses and Yemeni officials confirmed a civilian also died in the attack.

The incident marked the third drone strike of 2014 in Yemen and the second in the southeastern province of Hadramout.

## Context on Drone Policy

The article references President Obama's May 2013 statement establishing rules for drone strikes, stating strikes should meet "near-certainty that no civilians will be killed or injured." However, Bureau analysis found that despite fewer strikes following this policy announcement, overall death tolls from drone operations increased.

## Related Concerns

The strike occurred days after reports emerged of a December 2013 wedding party attack that killed 6-15 civilians, later confirmed as a Joint Special Operations Command (JSOC) operation. This contributed to Yemen recording the highest civilian casualty rate per confirmed strike in 2013.
