title: WATCH: Yemeni tells US Senate 'drones are fuelling anti-Americanism'
date: April 24, 2013
authors: Alice Ross
content:

Yemeni journalist and activist Farea al-Muslimi delivered compelling testimony before a rare US Senate subcommittee hearing on targeted killing and drone strikes. He provided a firsthand account of how aerial operations have impacted Yemen.

## Key Points

**Al-Muslimi's Central Message:**
The witness stated that "Drone strikes are the face of America to many Yemenis," emphasizing how these operations shape perceptions in his homeland.

**Documented Civilian Casualties:**
Al-Muslimi referenced the December 2009 al-Majala cruise missile strike, which killed over 40 civilians including children and pregnant women. He described the devastating aftermath in graphic terms.

**Recent Personal Impact:**
A reported drone strike on April 18 near his village left residents "scared and angry," profoundly affecting him emotionally.

**Challenge to Official Claims:**
He contested assertions by President Obama and CIA Director John Brennan that targeted killings occur only as a "last resort," suggesting the presumed target could have been arrested instead.

**Hearing Context:**
The Senate constitutional subcommittee also heard from retired General James Cartwright, constitutional lawyers, and casualty-tracking organizations, with most witnesses expressing reservations about current drone operations.
