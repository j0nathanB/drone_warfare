title: Arab spring brings steep rise in US attacks in Yemen
date: March 29, 2012
authors: Chris Woods, Emma Slater
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that covert U.S. military and CIA operations in Yemen have escalated dramatically during the Arab Spring, reaching levels comparable to the drone campaign in Pakistan. Their investigation identified at least 27 strikes involving cruise missiles, aircraft, drones, or naval bombardments that killed hundreds of alleged militants but also resulted in approximately 55 civilian deaths.

## Key Findings

**Scale of Operations:**
- 27-45 documented strikes since 2001, with most occurring after May 2011
- Estimated 280-522 total killed; 55-105 civilians reported dead
- March 2012 alone saw at least six U.S. attacks

**Notable Incidents:**

A December 2009 cluster missile strike on an alleged al Qaeda gathering killed 14 militants but also 44 civilians, including 22 children. A parliamentary commission report identified all victims by name, but the U.S. has neither confirmed nor investigated the incident.

**Attribution Challenges:**

An AirForces Monthly correspondent noted that "Yemen's air force appears incapable of night operations," suggesting American involvement in strikes attributed to Yemen's military.

**Political Context:**

Operations intensified after President Abed Rabbo Mansour Hadi's 2012 inauguration, during which he pledged to continue "war against al-Qaida as a religious and national duty."
