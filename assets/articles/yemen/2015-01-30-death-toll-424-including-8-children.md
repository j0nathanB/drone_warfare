title: Analysis: What next for Yemen as death toll from confirmed US drone strikes hits 424, including 8 children
date: January 30, 2015
authors: Jack Serle
content:

A CIA drone strike in Yemen on January 26, 2015, killed three individuals, including a child reportedly between 12-15 years old. This brought the confirmed death toll from US drone operations in Yemen since 2002 to at least 424 people, including eight children.

One victim was identified as Mohammed Toayman, whose father and brother had been killed in a previous 2011 strike. According to Yemeni journalist Amal al Yarisi, the boy was "studying in the 6th grade" and had no involvement with al Qaeda. His family contested claims he was an operative.

## Yemen's Political Crisis

The strike occurred amid severe governmental collapse. Yemen's president, prime minister, and cabinet all resigned on January 22 after Houthi rebels stormed the presidential palace, following months of occupation of the capital Sanaa.

Adam Baron from the European Council on Foreign Relations warned this chaos created opportunities for extremist groups, stating the situation represented "a fantastic opportunity for al Qaeda."

## Broader Context

The article details how political instability since 2011 enabled al Qaeda in the Arab Peninsula (AQAP) to expand control in southern Yemen. As government institutions weakened, the militant group filled gaps by providing services and security. With economic collapse now threatening, AQAP's ability to pay salaries could further increase its influence among desperate populations.
