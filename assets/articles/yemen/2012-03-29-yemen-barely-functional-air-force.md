title: Yemen's 'Barely Functional' Air Force Points to US Involvement in Strikes
date: March 29, 2012
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reveals evidence suggesting the US conducted airstrikes in Yemen attributed to the Yemeni Air Force. Despite Yemen being a key US counterterrorism ally, investigation shows the country's air force lacks the capability to execute these operations independently.

## Key Findings

**Operational Incapacity**

The Yemeni Air Force reportedly cannot conduct precision strikes or nighttime missions. According to analysis, at least eight airstrikes against alleged al Qaeda targets killed approximately 102 people, yet the force lacks necessary equipment and training. Yemeni analyst Abdul Ghani Iryani states the air force "has not been functioning in ages" due to pervasive corruption.

**Aircraft Limitations**

Yemen possesses 79 combat-capable aircraft including 16 MiG-29s, 15 F-5 fighters, and aged MiG-21s. However, many remain inoperable—some F-5 engines were cannibalized for spare parts. Aviation Week journalist Sharon Weinberger described the fleet as "a hodge-podge mix of former Soviet equipment with some from the US," with maintenance and spare parts presenting constant challenges.

**Personnel Challenges**

In January 2012, approximately 2,000 of the 3,000-member air force went on strike protesting corruption and nepotism. Pilots cannot conduct night operations lacking navigation instruments due to military procurement corruption.

**Official Deception**

A WikiLeaks diplomatic cable documents former President Ali Abdullah Saleh telling General David Petraeus: "We'll continue saying the bombs are ours, not yours."
