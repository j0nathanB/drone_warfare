title: Radical cleric al-Awlaki 'killed in Yemen drone strike'
date: September 30, 2011
authors: Chris Woods
content:

Anwar al-Awlaki, a U.S.-born radical cleric with alleged ties to al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula, was killed in a Yemen airstrike. According to Yemen's Defence Ministry, "the terrorist Anwar al-Awlaki has been killed along with some of his companions" in Jawf province. U.S. counter-terrorism officials confirmed the death, with media reports indicating the CIA conducted the operation using drone strikes.

## Key Details

**Background on al-Awlaki:**
- Sometimes incorrectly described as AQAP leader; held mid-level command position
- Fluent English speaker whose sermons allegedly inspired homegrown U.S. Islamic militants
- Initially cleared by FBI regarding 9/11 involvement, though later suspected of possible al Qaeda operational involvement
- Two original 9/11 hijackers attended his mosque

**Timeline:**
- Left U.S. in 2002; spent two years in London before moving to Yemen
- Arrested by Yemeni authorities in 2006; released a year later despite FBI interviews
- December 2009: Failed U.S. covert operation killed ~30 others
- May 2011: Botched drone strike; al-Awlaki later mocked the attempt in jihadist magazine *Inspire*

**Legal Concerns:**
The American Civil Liberties Union condemned the operation, noting citizens could be "executed by their own government without judicial process" based on undisclosed standards.
