title: US Strike Kills Australian and New Zealander in Yemen
date: April 17, 2014
authors: Alice Ross, Jack Serle
content:

Australia and New Zealand confirmed that two of their citizens died in a US military operation in Yemen in November 2013. The victims were Christopher Harvard, an Australian from Townsville, Queensland, and a dual Australian-New Zealand national known as Muslim bin John.

The strike occurred on November 19, 2013, in Hadramout province as the men traveled by vehicle. Up to five people died in total, including three others: Abu Habib al Yemeni and Wadhah al Hadhrami (both Yemeni) and Hamam al Masri (Egyptian).

Harvard and bin John were reportedly junior members of al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula and were identified through DNA analysis. A counter-terrorism source indicated they "may have been collateral damage," suggesting they were unintended targets. The intended target was reportedly Abu Habib al Yemeni.

The operation was conducted solely by US forces—either the CIA or Joint Special Operations Command. Both governments stated they had no involvement or prior knowledge of the strike.

New Zealand Prime Minister John Key noted that "my intelligence agencies informed me" of bin John's death, though he acknowledged uncertainty about intelligence sources.

The article also discusses an earlier suspected case of Australian Saifullah, reportedly killed in a 2011 Pakistan drone strike, which the Australian government initially denied before later stating there was insufficient evidence of Australian involvement.
