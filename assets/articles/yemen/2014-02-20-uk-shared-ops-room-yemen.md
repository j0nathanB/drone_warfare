title: UK shared ops room where drone targets were identified – Yemen president
date: February 20, 2014
authors: Jack Serle, Alice Ross
content:

Yemen's president asserted that the UK participates in a secret joint operations control room in Sana'a where potential drone targets are identified. The facility reportedly includes representatives from the US, Yemen, and NATO, according to President Abdel Rabbo Mansur al Hadi's statements to Human Rights Watch researchers.

A Yemeni government official characterized the room's function as focused on "intelligence-sharing activities" rather than solely counter-terrorism operations. However, a NATO official denied the organization's involvement, stating: "There are no NATO personnel on the ground in Yemen; NATO is not participating in any intelligence, military or counter-terrorism actions inside Yemen."

The UK Foreign Office declined to address the allegations, citing its "longstanding policy of successive governments not to comment on intelligence matters."

The revelations emerged during HRW's investigation into a December 2013 drone strike that struck a wedding procession near Rada'a, killing approximately 12 individuals. HRW researchers identified survivors and family members who contradicted official claims that all casualties were militants.

Human Rights Watch researcher Letta Tayler emphasized: "It raises significant questions as to the role of governments such as United Kingdom in intelligence-sharing in Yemen."

Human rights organization Reprieve subsequently filed a complaint with the International Criminal Court, alleging NATO allies—specifically the UK, Germany, and Australia—bear responsibility for US drone operations.
