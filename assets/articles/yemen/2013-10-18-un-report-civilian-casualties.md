title: US Must Release Data on Civilian Drone Casualties, Says UN Report
date: October 18, 2013
authors: Alice Ross
content:

A UN expert has called for the United States to publicly disclose information about civilian casualties from drone strikes, arguing that national security considerations should not prevent transparency on basic statistical data.

## Key Points

Ben Emmerson, a British barrister serving as UN special rapporteur on counter-terrorism, released a major report examining drone operations by the US, Britain, and Israel across multiple regions including Afghanistan, Pakistan, Yemen, Somalia, Iraq, Libya, and Gaza.

**Main Findings:**

- Emmerson stated: "The single greatest obstacle to an evaluation of the civilian impact of drone strikes is lack of transparency"
- Drones currently operate in an "accountability vacuum" with inadequate investigation into civilian casualty claims
- CIA involvement in drone operations has created "an almost insurmountable obstacle to transparency"
- The report identifies 33 strikes appearing to cause civilian casualties requiring accountability review

**Casualty Data:**

Pakistan provided figures showing at least 400 civilian deaths with 200 additional probable non-combatants. For Yemen, the report cited estimates of 21-58 civilian casualties but noted insufficient official data for other regions.

**Recommendations:**

Emmerson emphasized that states have legal obligations to investigate civilian casualty claims from credible sources, including NGOs, with results made public subject to legitimate security redactions.

Human rights organizations including Reprieve criticized the US for withholding information about covert drone programs.
