title: Nine Young Children Killed: The Full Details of Botched US Raid in Yemen
date: February 9, 2017
authors: Namir Shabibi, Nasser al Sane
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism documents a failed US military operation in Yemen's al Bayda province on January 29, 2017. The raid, intended to target al Qaeda leadership, resulted in significant civilian casualties including nine children under age 13.

## Key Details

**The Operation:** US Navy SEALS conducted a ground assault on the village of al Yakla following months of planning and rehearsals. The team departed from the USS Makin Island aboard Osprey aircraft. One aircraft crash-landed, injuring three troops immediately.

**Casualties:** Villagers reported 25 civilian deaths alongside militants. The nine child victims ranged from three months to 12 years old, including:
- Asma Fahad Ali al Ameri (3 months)
- Nawar Anwar al Awlaqi (8 years), daughter of radical cleric Anwar al Awlaqi

Eight women died, including one pregnant woman. Seven additional women and children sustained injuries.

**What Went Wrong:** Soldiers opened fire on residents fleeing their homes. Helicopter gunships subsequently fired on civilians and structures indiscriminately, according to eyewitness accounts from the surviving community.

**Official Response:** The White House characterized the operation as "highly successful," claiming it achieved its intelligence-gathering objectives despite acknowledging one Navy SEAL death and destruction of a $70 million aircraft.

**Broader Impact:** Experts noted the raid potentially strengthened AQAP recruitment narratives, undermining US counterterrorism objectives in the region.
