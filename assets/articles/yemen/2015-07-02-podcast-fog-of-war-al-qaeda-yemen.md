title: Podcast: Amid the fog of war, do we actually know who belongs to al Qaeda in Yemen now?
date: July 2, 2015
authors: Abigail Fielding-Smith
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published a podcast episode discussing the difficulty of identifying al Qaeda members in Yemen amid ongoing conflict.

**Key participants:**
- Iona Craig, correspondent with four years of Yemen experience
- Amrit Singh, Open Society Justice Initiative researcher

**Main points:**

Craig explained that distinguishing between actual militant members and those merely connected to them has become increasingly ambiguous. She noted that "al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP) is very much integrated in to a lot of the local tribes," observing that providing shelter or transportation doesn't necessarily indicate willingness to fight.

The civil war intensified this confusion. Craig stated that "anybody that is against the Houthis or wanting to fight the Houthis they have been joining forces with" AQAP "deliberately not calling themselves al Qaeda."

Singh highlighted consequences of drone strikes, noting that "the victim community expressed considerable outrage both at the Yemeni government and at the United States" in documented cases.
