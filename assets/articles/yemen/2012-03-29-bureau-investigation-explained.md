title: Yemen: The Bureau's Investigation Explained
date: March 29, 2012
authors: Emma Slater
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published research revealing an escalation in covert US military strikes against alleged militants in Yemen during the Arab Spring period. The investigation was co-published with the Daily Telegraph and Al Jazeera English.

### Key Findings

The research documented "at least 26 US military strikes" in Yemen that resulted in hundreds of alleged militant casualties alongside "at least 54 civilians" killed. A significant temporal pattern emerged: 75% of attacks occurred after May 2011, coinciding with Arab Spring unrest.

By March 2012, the strike frequency matched CIA drone operations in Pakistan, with five strikes documented in Yemen that month alone compared to three in Pakistan during the same period.

### Context

The strikes targeted individuals described as linked to the regional Al Qaeda franchise. The Bureau's investigation highlighted the covert nature of these operations and their escalation during a period of regional political upheaval, presenting comparative data on US counterterrorism activities across multiple theaters.
