title: Podcast: A changing drone conflict in Pakistan and Yemen
date: August 23, 2014
authors: Bureau Reporter
content:

This podcast episode from The Bureau of Investigative Journalism examines evolving drone strike patterns in Pakistan and Yemen through interviews with academic experts.

**Key Points:**

According to Dr. Wali Aslam of the University of Bath, US drone strikes in Pakistan operate under a "tacit agreement" where each nation assists in eliminating the other's adversaries. He notes Pakistan provides "key" intelligence on al Qaeda operations in exchange for US drone support during military offensives in North Waziristan.

The Bureau observed that recent strikes targeted territory controlled by Hafiz Gul Bahadur, a warlord who had signed a peace accord with Pakistan's government. A tribal elder suggested this agreement explained why drones conducted attacks rather than ground forces.

Professor Stefan Wolff discusses Yemen operations, noting drone strikes against al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP) have succeeded tactically but represent "not so much a policy of choice, but of necessity" given the absence of ground troops for broader engagement.

The episode features interviews conducted in July at Birmingham University's Institute for Conflict, Cooperation and Security.
