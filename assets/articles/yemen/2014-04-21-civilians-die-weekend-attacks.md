title: Civilians die in reported Yemen drone strike as weekend of attacks kills at least 35
date: April 21, 2014
authors: Alice Ross
content:

A weekend of airstrikes in Yemen resulted in at least 35 deaths, including a major drone strike that killed between 10-21 people. The attack targeted a truck allegedly carrying al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP) members but also struck a civilian vehicle, killing at least three civilians described as construction workers or laborers.

## Key Details

The Saturday strike represented the highest confirmed death toll from any drone strike in Yemen that year. Eyewitness Salem al Kushn reported: "Our vehicle was 15 meters from the attacked pickup, and the shrapnel from the strike poured on our car."

Sunday attacks on suspected militant camps in the same province resulted in conflicting casualty reports, ranging from five to over 30 deaths. These remain classified as "possible" rather than confirmed drone strikes.

## Official Statements and Analysis

A "high-level Yemeni government official" told CNN the operation was joint U.S.-Yemeni, adding: "Unfortunately, a civilian truck was also hit."

The article notes President Obama's 2013 pledge of "near-certainty that no civilians will be killed." ACLU attorney Hina Shamsi questioned the strike's consistency with that standard, stating the attack "couldn't take place after a car full of civilians was out of danger."
