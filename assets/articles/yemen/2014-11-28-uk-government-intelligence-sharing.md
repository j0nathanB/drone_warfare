title: UK Government Urged to Publish Intelligence-Sharing Guidance
date: November 28, 2014
authors: The Bureau of Investigative Journalism
content:

A former GCHQ director has called on the government to disclose legal guidance on intelligence sharing with the US, expressing concerns about potential UK involvement in American drone strikes.

Sir David Omand, along with prominent parliamentarians including Tom Watson and David Davis (leaders of the All Party Parliamentary Group on Drones), sent a letter to Foreign Secretary Philip Hammond requesting transparency on intelligence protocols.

**Key concern:** The signatories worry that the UK's close intelligence collaboration with the US could "inadvertently" make Britain complicit in targeted killings that may violate international law.

The letter specifically requested disclosure of "the Guidance to Intelligence Officers and Service Personnel applicable to the passing of intelligence relating to individuals who are at risk of targeted lethal strikes."

An FCO spokesperson declined to address the matter, stating: "we do not comment on intelligence issues."

**Background context:** A Birmingham Policy Commission report highlighted distinctions between drone operations in established conflict zones (Iraq, Afghanistan) versus covert US campaigns in Yemen, Somalia, and Pakistan. Ben Emmerson QC, leading a UN drone investigation, previously stated it was "inevitable" that British intelligence had supported US drone operations.

The letter's signatories sought disclosure to "reassure an anxious public" regarding protection from inadvertent participation in potentially unlawful counter-terrorism activities.
