title: US Drone Shot Down Over Yemen
date: October 2, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Abigail Fielding-Smith, Jack Serle
content:

The United States confirmed that one of its MQ-9 Reaper combat drones was shot down in western Yemen on October 1, 2017, near Sanaa. US Central Command stated the incident was under investigation.

Yemen's state news agency SABA, controlled by the Houthi militia, first reported the attack. The Houthis, aligned with Iran, control much of Yemen following their 2014 seizure of power. Saudi Arabia leads a coalition conducting military intervention, supported by US intelligence.

The ongoing civil war has created a humanitarian catastrophe. According to the article, "the civil war has killed 10,000 people" and displaced millions, while also triggering a cholera epidemic affecting hundreds of thousands of Yemenis.

The weapon responsible remains unclear—it could be a remnant from Yemen's Soviet-supplied air defense system or Iranian-supplied weaponry. Most of Yemen's original surface-to-air missiles were reportedly destroyed by Saudi coalition airstrikes.

The US deploys Reapers for both surveillance and strikes. The article notes the US "carried out more than 100 strikes by drones as well as manned jets in Yemen this year," surpassing four previous years combined.
