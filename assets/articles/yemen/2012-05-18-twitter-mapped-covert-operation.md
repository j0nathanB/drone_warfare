title: How Twitter Mapped a 'Covert' US Drone Operation in Yemen
date: May 18, 2012
authors: Chris Woods, Jack Serle
content:

This article documents how social media users in Yemen provided real-time coverage of a US drone strike, undermining the operation's supposed secrecy. On May 17, 2012, a missile strike killed three alleged militants in Wadi Hadhramaut near Shibam, but Twitter users had already begun reporting the attack within minutes.

## Key Events

Sanaa-based lawyer Haykal Bafana alerted his followers about suspicious drone activity in the region before the strike occurred. After the attack, he tweeted: "Missile strike on car in Wadi Hadhramaut. Near city of Shibam. Suspected US drone attack." Bafana explained that his relatives witnessed the explosion and immediately contacted him.

The incident marked what experts believe was the first instance of people tweeting about an active drone operation in real-time. Dr. Micah Zenko of the Council on Foreign Policy noted this "really gets to the myth that you can keep these strikes covert."

## Broader Context

The article highlights how social media activism in Yemen, which played a role in the Arab Spring, is now being used to oppose drone operations. The #NoDrones hashtag trended when President Obama's counterterrorism adviser visited Sanaa, with activists expressing concerns about civilian casualties and radicalization effects.

The piece also references a precedent: Pakistani IT consultant Sohaib Athar unknowingly live-tweeted the 2011 Osama bin Laden raid.
