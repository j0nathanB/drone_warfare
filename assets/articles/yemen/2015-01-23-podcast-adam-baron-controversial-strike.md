title: Podcast: Yemen expert Adam Baron discusses controversial drone strike
date: January 23, 2015
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism released a podcast episode featuring Yemen expert Adam Baron discussing U.S. drone operations in the country. Baron, who spent three years reporting from Yemen for publications including McClatchy and Foreign Policy, shared his perspectives on the strategic and political implications of the drone campaign.

Baron emphasized that drone strikes appear to "increase anti-American sentiment among ordinary Yemenis" and highlighted challenges surrounding U.S. intelligence gathering efforts in the region.

The podcast focused particularly on a November 2012 strike that killed Andan al Qadhi, a former military officer from a prominent family, in the home village of ex-president Ali Abdullah Saleh. Baron described this operation as "very controversial," noting that Yemenis believed authorities could have pursued judicial means rather than lethal action. He suggested the strike "does not appear to pass the test that the Obama administration laid out for a drone strike."

The episode also covered reporting about strain within U.S. drone pilot ranks and operational pressures on the military's drone fleet.

**Note:** The interview was recorded January 15, 2015, one week before Yemen's president resigned following Houthi separatist actions.
