title: US Airstrike on al Qaeda Training Camp 'Kills 50' – Biggest Pentagon Attack in Yemen for Five Years
date: March 23, 2016
authors: Jack Serle, Abigail Fielding-Smith
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported on a significant U.S. military operation in Yemen targeting an al Qaeda training facility. According to local sources, between 40-50 fighters were killed in the strike on Hadramaut province, making it the deadliest American attack in Yemen within five years.

## Key Details

The Pentagon officially announced responsibility for the operation—notably the first public DoD acknowledgment of a Yemen strike. Witnesses described the attack occurring as "al Qaeda people stood in line to receive their dinner meal."

Death toll estimates varied: Reuters reported 50 killed with approximately 30 wounded, while AFP cited 40 casualties. This attack represented the most lethal operation since December 2009, when cruise missiles killed at least 55 civilians.

## Broader Context

The 2016 strike marked the sixth U.S. operation in Yemen that year, with at least 68 fatalities overall. Since President Obama took office in 2009, there have been approximately 128 counter-terrorism strikes across Yemen, killing roughly 714 people, including 133 reported civilians.

Yemen analyst Adam Baron suggested the unusually intense assault indicated American concerns about al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula's expanding territorial control following their seizure of Mukalla.
