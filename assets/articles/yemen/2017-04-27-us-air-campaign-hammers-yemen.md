title: Trump's 100 days: US air campaign hammers Yemen with almost a strike a day
date: April 27, 2017
authors: Jack Serle
content:

During President Trump's first 100 days in office, the US military dramatically escalated air operations in Yemen. "More US strikes have hit Yemen in President Donald Trump's first 100 days in office than in all of 2015 and 2016 combined."

In March and April alone, the US conducted 80 air attacks using jets and drones—more than double the total from the previous year. This surge followed Trump's decision in late January to exempt Yemen from Obama's Presidential Policy Guidance, removing civilian casualty protections and allowing US Central Command generals to authorize strikes without White House oversight.

## Regional Impact

The article documents significant increases across multiple theaters:

- **Yemen:** 80 strikes in two months
- **Pakistan:** CIA drone operations resumed after a nine-month halt, with two confirmed strikes
- **Afghanistan:** Over 600 air strikes as of mid-April, including the April 13 deployment of the MOAB bomb against ISIS-K
- **Somalia:** Areas declared active hostilities, though Africa Command maintained stricter engagement rules

## Civilian Casualties

A January 29 ground raid in Yemen resulted in at least 25 civilian deaths, with findings confirmed by Human Rights Watch and The Intercept.
