title: Podcast: What Do Hostage Deaths in Pakistan Mean for US Drone Policy?
date: April 29, 2015
authors: Abigail Fielding-Smith
content:

This piece covers a podcast discussion by The Bureau of Investigative Journalism's drone team regarding President Obama's acknowledgment that a CIA drone strike in Pakistan killed two Western hostages held by al Qaeda.

**The Incident:**
The January strike targeted a compound and resulted in the deaths of American aid worker Warren Weinstein and Italian aid worker Giovanni Lo Porto, along with a militant named Ahmed Farouq.

**Key Discussion Points:**

The journalists examined how this strike represented a "signature strike"—where targets are selected based on behavioral patterns rather than confirmed identity. The team noted the administration had previously signaled moving away from this targeting method.

Notably, the CIA's Pakistan program reportedly received a waiver exempting it from Obama's 2013 standard requiring targets to pose an "imminent threat" to the United States.

The discussion highlighted operational differences: "60% of the strikes have hit buildings" in Pakistan, contrasting with Afghanistan's policy treating "all compounds as assumed to house civilians unless proven clear," and Yemen's rare targeting of buildings.

Despite this incident, no confirmed Pakistani civilian casualties had been documented since 2012 prior to this revelation.
