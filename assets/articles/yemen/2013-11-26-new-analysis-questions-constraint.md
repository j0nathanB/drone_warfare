title: New analysis questions constraint on US drone strikes
date: November 26, 2013
authors: Jack Serle
content:

Six months after President Obama announced new guidelines for armed drone operations, the Bureau of Investigative Journalism found that covert strikes in Yemen and Pakistan had actually killed more people than in the previous six-month period, despite a decrease in the number of strikes.

### Key Findings

**Yemen Operations:**
The analysis revealed that all confirmed U.S. drone strikes in the six months following the speech occurred within a 15-day period in late July and August, consisting of eight attacks. This bombardment proved more intense than any comparable period since mid-2012. At least 29 people died, though only three were identified as significant al Qaeda leaders. The strikes killed an estimated 6-7 civilians, including children.

**Pakistan Operations:**
Pakistan saw 13 strikes in the six months after the speech, compared to 18 in the preceding six months. However, each strike killed more people on average—rising from 3.5 to nearly five casualties per strike. No confirmed civilian deaths were reported during this period.

### Context

Obama stated that drone use required "near-certainty that no civilians will be killed or injured," yet casualties continued post-speech. The analysis questions how effectively the new policy constrained operations, particularly regarding controversial "signature strikes" targeting unidentified individuals based on behavior patterns.
