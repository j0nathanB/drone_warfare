title: Suspected drone strikes kill 12 civilians in Yemen
date: May 15, 2012
authors: Jack Serle
content:

Two suspected US drone strikes on a house in Jaar, southern Yemen killed between 14-15 people, with as many as 12 reported as civilians and up to 21 injured.

Witnesses described the first strike targeting alleged militants in a meeting, followed by a second strike that hit civilians who had rushed to assist at the impact site. This pattern echoes documented US drone tactics previously investigated in Pakistan.

## Key Details

The Bureau had earlier exposed CIA "follow-up" strike practices, where twin strikes killed at least 50 civilians when rescue workers arrived at initial attack sites. Though unconfirmed, the Yemen incident appeared to follow this methodology.

Between 2-3 suspected al Qaeda militants were reportedly killed. Initial reports attributed the strikes to Yemeni warplanes, but three Yemeni security officials later confirmed to CNN it was a drone strike.

This represented the highest civilian casualty toll from a US-attributed strike in Yemen since July 2011, when 30 people died in a police station strike.

## Broader Context

The Bureau documented approximately 746 people killed in US strikes throughout Yemen since 2002, including roughly 117 civilians and 24 children. The article also covered EU anti-piracy operations against Somalia and US counterterrorism activities in the region.
