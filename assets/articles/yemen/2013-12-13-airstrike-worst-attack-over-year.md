title: Airstrike in Yemen Worst Attack in Over a Year
date: December 13, 2013
authors: Jack Serle
content:

A reported US drone attack on a wedding convoy in southern Yemen killed between 10-17 people, marking the deadliest airstrike in the country that year. The incident occurred in Radaa, capital of al Bayda province, striking four to five vehicles in a procession of up to 11.

## Key Details

Initial reports conflicted regarding casualties. Some outlets reported civilian deaths, while others, including the New York Times, indicated al Qaeda members were present at the wedding party. Local media identified up to 19 deceased individuals, with approximately four confirmed as militant operatives and the remainder described as sheikhs and tribal members from the al Amer and al Tays families.

The strike left "charred bodies and burnt out cars on the road," according to Associated Press reporting.

## Context

If confirmed as a drone operation, this would represent the first documented drone strike targeting a wedding party across Yemen, Pakistan, or Somalia based on Bureau records. The casualty count exceeded a previous December 2012 incident in the same location where 12 civilians died returning from a market.

President Obama had outlined stricter rules months earlier, stating that strikes require "near-certainty that no civilians will be killed or injured."

## Response

The US declined official comment, with an unnamed official telling Reuters they had "no information that corroborates these reports."

Human Rights Watch researchers called for greater US transparency regarding the incident.
