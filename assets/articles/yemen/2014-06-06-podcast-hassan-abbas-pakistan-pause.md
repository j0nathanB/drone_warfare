title: Podcast: Hassan Abbas on militancy and the pause in Pakistan drone strikes
date: June 6, 2014
authors: Bureau Reporter
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism published a podcast featuring Hassan Abbas, a National Defense University professor specializing in national security and militancy, discussing Pakistan's drone strike pause and its impact on regional stability.

## Key Points

**The Drone Strike Hiatus**
A six-month pause in drone strikes began after Christmas 2013, following a Pakistani government request to suspend operations and allow peace talks with the Taliban to proceed.

**Militancy Continues Despite Pause**
Abbas emphasizes that "there has been no corresponding decrease in the number of suicide attacks" in the tribal belt, contradicting claims that terrorist violence responds directly to drone operations.

**Strategic Concerns**
Abbas critiques how Western policy developed: "The problem was that in the west, this tactic was converted into a strategy, which was bad policy."

**Rumored Extensions**
According to Abbas, Washington insiders suggest the suspension could extend 12-18 months to allow Pakistan to address the Taliban threat independently through negotiations or military action.

**Broader Context**
The podcast also covered Obama's foreign policy speech, Australian public anger over a citizen's death in a drone strike, and related international developments.
