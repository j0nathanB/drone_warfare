title: Podcast: CIA torture report dissected by Reprieve's Crofton Black
date: December 12, 2014
authors: Bureau Reporter
content:

In this Drone News episode, Owen Bennett-Jones interviews Crofton Black from the legal NGO Reprieve about the Senate's CIA torture report. Black discusses the shocking findings regarding the agency's secret detention program.

### Key Points

Black expressed surprise at "the graphic and horrific level of abuse" and the "extraordinary level of mismanagement" revealed in the report. He highlighted systemic failures, noting that "objections being raised were never passed up the chain of command" and there were instances of intentional oversight obstruction.

A notable finding: approximately 25% of prisoners held in the program didn't meet the CIA's own inclusion criteria.

### Abu Zubaydah Case

The report focused extensively on Abu Zubaydah, around whom "the whole programme was structured." Initially believed to be al Qaeda's third-ranking official, he was subjected to extreme measures including prolonged confinement. Black emphasized the "terrible irony" that Zubaydah wasn't actually number three in al Qaeda. He remains detained at Guantanamo Bay without charges or trial, with no apparent government plans for release.

### Additional Coverage

Jack Serle and Abigail Fielding-Smith updated listeners on drone operations in Pakistan and Yemen, including recent developments in hostage rescue attempts and unconfirmed strike reports.
