title: Bloodiest US and Yemeni attacks in two years kill at least 40 people
date: April 22, 2014
authors: Patrick Galey, Jack Serle, Alice Ross
content:

Three coordinated operations in Yemen—involving US drone strikes and Yemeni forces—resulted in at least 40 deaths over a single weekend, marking the deadliest period since March 2012. The strikes targeted suspected al Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP) members across multiple provinces.

## Key Details

**The Attacks:**
- Two US drone strikes and a Yemeni special forces operation killed dozens of people between April 19-21, 2014
- At least three civilians were confirmed killed—only the second civilian casualty incident reported that year
- The Yemeni Ministry of Interior claimed approximately 55 suspected AQAP militants were eliminated

**Documented Incidents:**

On April 19, a drone strike in Al-Bayda Province hit two vehicles, killing between 13-21 people, including civilians described as construction workers. One survivor reported: "a rocket hit the car which was 15 meters away from us."

April 20 saw strikes on three suspected militant camps in the same province, with between 25-30 casualties reported.

An April 21 operation in Shabwa Province killed three people in a vehicle.

## Controversy

Human rights advocates questioned whether the strikes aligned with President Obama's stated policy requiring "near-certainty" of no civilian casualties before strikes proceed. The American Civil Liberties Union noted the difficulty in justifying the strikes given civilian presence in the area.
