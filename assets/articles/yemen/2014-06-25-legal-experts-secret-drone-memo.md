title: Legal Experts Dissect the US Government's Secret Drone Memo: A Round-Up
date: June 25, 2014
authors: Alice Ross
content:

A US court ordered release of a classified memo justifying the targeted killing of American citizen Anwar al-Awlaki in a CIA drone strike in Yemen in September 2011. The Office of Legal Counsel document, written in July 2010, outlined the government's legal rationale for the operation.

## Key Arguments in the Memo

The document asserted that US military personnel could legally target Awlaki under a "public authority justification"—allowing government actors to perform otherwise illegal acts during emergencies. The memo relied on the Authorization for the Use of Military Force to argue the US was at war with al-Qaeda and affiliated groups.

The memo claimed Awlaki posed a "continued and imminent threat" sufficient to override his constitutional due process rights as a US citizen.

## Expert Analysis

**Criticisms:**

- The *New York Times* called it a "hastily assembled collection of legal theories clearly designed to reach a predetermined outcome."

- Kevin Jon Heller noted the CIA analysis was "probably the most inadequate part" and "not very convincing," suggesting the memo focused primarily on Defense Department authority.

- Steve Vladeck observed the analysis was "remarkably narrow," addressing only case-specific facts rather than establishing broader principles.

- Brett Kaufman highlighted the absence of "post-facto judicial review" and vague evidentiary standards for determining threat levels.

**Supportive Perspectives:**

Ryan Goodman credited the memo for imposing "principled legal constraints on warfighting and covert action" while adhering to international law standards.

## Unresolved Questions

Analysts identified significant gaps: undefined standards for "imminent threat," unclear criteria for determining organizational affiliation with al-Qaeda, and the absence of capture feasibility assessments.

Awlaki's 16-year-old son, Abdulrahman, was killed in a separate drone strike two weeks later.
