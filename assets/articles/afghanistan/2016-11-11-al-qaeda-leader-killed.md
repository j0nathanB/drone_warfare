title: Al Qaeda leader killed in US strike in Afghanistan
date: November 11, 2016
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

The Pentagon confirmed that senior al Qaeda leader Faruq al Qatani died in a "precision strike" on October 23 in Afghanistan's Kunar province. A subsequent strike that same day reportedly killed Bilal al Utabi, another senior al Qaeda operative, though the US has not officially verified his death.

The US military stated the strikes targeted "command-and-control locations" following "extensive surveillance." Both individuals were believed to be attempting to restore safe havens for al Qaeda in Afghanistan, with al Qatani serving as the organization's leader in northeastern Afghanistan. American forces had pursued al Qatani for four years.

Al Qatani's death was added to the Bureau's registry of alleged terrorists and militants killed in US strikes. Since 2015, when the US became the sole nation conducting fast jet and drone operations in Afghanistan, the Bureau documented 123 alleged militants eliminated in US strikes there.

The Bureau noted verification challenges, as operatives sometimes resurface months later. This contrasts sharply with Pakistan, where only two alleged militants killed by US strikes since 2015 have been identified by name. Prior efforts (2004-2014) identified 302 alleged militants killed there. Pakistan drone operations have largely ceased since May 2016.

Afghanistan strikes have intensified after slowing by late 2014. President Obama modified rules in June 2016, permitting expanded military targeting of Taliban insurgents. Strike frequency increased substantially—from roughly two daily to six daily by August, then tripling thereafter. The US additionally pursues ISIS operations in Nangarhar province.
