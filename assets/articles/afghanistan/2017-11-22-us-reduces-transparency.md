title: US reduces transparency while escalating air war in Afghanistan
date: November 22, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that it has been unable to obtain monthly strike data from Resolute Support, the U.S.-led NATO mission in Afghanistan, for the first time since beginning to receive such information in September 2016.

The missing October data represents a concerning gap in transparency. The organization had previously received "monthly strike totals...broken down by strike types," detailing operations against ISIS and al-Qaeda affiliates in Afghanistan.

According to the report, "It is not clear why the data has stopped. Resolute Support had said they would provide the figures on November 5 and again on November 8, but have failed to respond to more recent emails."

This transparency reduction is particularly troubling given the escalating air campaign. The Bureau notes that detailed provincial strike breakdowns provided in July and August have also ceased, with officials citing operational capacity constraints.

The timing is significant, as the withdrawal of data coincides with increased U.S. air operations and mounting civilian casualty allegations that the Bureau has previously documented.
