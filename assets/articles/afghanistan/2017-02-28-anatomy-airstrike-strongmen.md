title: Anatomy of a US airstrike: Are Afghan strongmen calling the shots?
date: February 28, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

This investigative piece examines a September 18, 2016 airstrike in Tarin Kot, Afghanistan that killed seven Afghan police officers. The incident highlights the chaos resulting from US reliance on local warlords and strongmen to combat Taliban insurgents.

**Key Events:**
Police commander Sadiqullah Sadaqat's men were manning a checkpoint when a US airstrike, ordered by General Abdul Raziq, struck the position. Sadaqat's brother and cousin were among those killed. Raziq claimed armed men were firing on highway traffic and posed a threat; Sadaqat contended the checkpoint was clearly marked as a government position with uniformed officers present.

**Central Conflict:**
The incident underscores tensions between regular Afghan security forces and irregular militia commanders. General Raziq, despite documented human rights violations, remains central to US strategy in southern Afghanistan. An unnamed US official previously characterized him as "a mad dog on a leash" necessary for regional stability.

**Broader Context:**
With Western troops withdrawn, Afghanistan's security increasingly depends on local strongmen operating outside formal command structures. As one former US commander noted: "There is no coordinated US-Afghan strategy for how to bring this war to a successful conclusion," creating conditions for further tragic incidents.
