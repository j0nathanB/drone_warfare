title: The Bureau identifies 15 civilians killed in a single US airstrike
date: November 4, 2016
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Ruhullah Khapalwak
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism documented 15 civilian deaths resulting from a US drone strike in Nangarhar province, Afghanistan on September 28, 2016. This represented the highest single-strike civilian casualty count since the October 2015 Médecins Sans Frontières hospital attack in Kunduz.

**Key Details:**

The United Nations quickly confirmed civilian casualties, stating the strike killed 15 and injured 13 people gathered at a house celebrating a tribal elder's return from pilgrimage. The US military had characterized the operation as targeting Islamic State fighters.

A homeowner named Haji Rais provided names of victims, which the Bureau independently verified. Casualties included the homeowner's son, a school headmaster, and three sons of another man, Abdul Hakim.

**Context:**

"The rate at which civilians are being killed by American airstrikes in Afghanistan is now higher than it was in 2014 when the US was engaged in active combat operations."

The article documents additional September incidents: eight Afghan policemen killed in Tarinkot, and approximately 22 Somali soldiers and civilians killed in Galcayo. The US initially denied non-combatant casualties in both cases and subsequently launched investigations.

The reporting represents part of the Bureau's "Naming the Dead" project.
