title: US airstrikes in Afghanistan killing civilians at greatest rate for seven years, new figures show
date: February 18, 2016
authors: Jack Serle, Abigail Fielding-Smith, Payenda Sargand
content:

The Bureau's analysis of UN data reveals that civilian casualties from US airstrikes in Afghanistan reached their highest rate since 2008. In 2015, one civilian died per four airstrikes on average, compared to one per eleven strikes in 2014.

**Key Findings:**
- 103 civilian deaths from US air actions in 2015 (versus 101 in 2014)
- 411 total airstrikes in 2015 (down from 1,136 in 2014)
- This represents the first increase in casualty rates since 2011

**Major Incidents:**
The Kunduz hospital strike by gunship in October accounted for 42 deaths. An additional 14 people were classified as civilian casualties in a counter-terrorism strike in Khost province in June, though the US disputes this designation.

**Expert Perspective:**
Airwars director Chris Woods stated that "hard-won" safety measures from 2009 onwards appear to be eroding, questioning "how many civilians is the US prepared to kill in Afghanistan to achieve its goals?"

**Context:**
Following General Stanley McChrystal's 2009 targeting reforms, casualty rates had declined annually until 2015. With ongoing Taliban advances and pressure to increase air support for Afghan forces, experts anticipate intensified conflict in 2016.
