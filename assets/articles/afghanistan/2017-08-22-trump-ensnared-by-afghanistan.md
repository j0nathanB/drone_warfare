title: Trump, like Obama, is ensnared by Afghanistan
date: August 22, 2017
authors: Abigail Fielding-Smith
content:

The piece examines how President Trump's escalation strategy in Afghanistan mirrors challenges faced by his predecessor. Key findings include:

**Military Activity:** "The US Air Force has dropped 1,984 missiles and bombs in Afghanistan in 2017" at nearly triple the rate compared to the same period the previous year.

**Historical Context:** Obama had intended to wind down operations, declaring combat officially over in 2014. However, as Taliban momentum increased, airstrikes surged by over 40% in 2016.

**Rule Changes:** Summer 2016 saw policy shifts allowing proactive strikes against militants rather than defensive operations only.

**Afghan Forces Challenges:** Despite "$70 billion worth of US spending," security forces struggle against the Taliban. Issues include systemic corruption and "ghost soldiers" on payrolls who don't exist.

**Counterterrorism Expansion:** The campaign now targets approximately 20 militant groups, including ISIS's Afghan franchise, which maintains an estimated 1,000 fighters.

**Civilian Impact:** UN data shows "a steadily climbing number of civilian casualties in recent years," though international forces account for just two percent, with militant groups causing the majority through IEDs and suicide attacks.

**Pakistan Factor:** Trump signaled pressure on Pakistan regarding militant safe havens, with four drone strikes recorded since taking office.
