title: US Air Force has carried out two strikes a day against Taliban since June
date: August 18, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The US military intensified air operations against the Taliban following new presidential directives in June 2016. According to data obtained by the Bureau, approximately 79 strikes occurred between late June and early August, averaging more than two daily attacks.

**Key Details:**

President Obama issued expanded rules on June 10, granting troops greater latitude to target insurgents and support Afghan forces. Previously, the military could only strike the Taliban defensively or prevent serious Afghan military setbacks.

The first strike under these new authorities occurred on June 24. Between that date and August 4, operations concentrated in Helmand province, where Taliban advances threatened the provincial capital.

**Broader Context:**

Since December 2014, most American forces participated in NATO's "train, advise, assist" mission. However, a smaller contingent conducted offensive operations against al Qaeda and Islamic State's Afghan branch.

General Charles Cleveland reported that approximately 500 total airstrikes occurred from January through August 9. Nearly 80 targeted Taliban positions under new authorities, while approximately 180 constituted counter-terrorism strikes against designated terrorist organizations.
