title: US drone war: Bureau launches new investigation into strikes in Afghanistan
date: February 27, 2015
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism announced a new investigation tracking US drone and air strikes in Afghanistan. As of early 2015, the organization had identified five confirmed American attacks allegedly resulting in 35-44 deaths among insurgents and militants.

Following NATO's transition from its combat mission to a training and advisory role, the US continues counterterrorism operations under "Operation Freedom Sentinel," launched January 1, 2015. This mission includes both air and drone strikes alongside special forces raids.

The Bureau aims to improve public accountability through systematic documentation, building on similar efforts in Pakistan, Somalia, and Yemen. However, obtaining comprehensive information about Afghan strikes presents challenges due to journalist safety concerns and limited telecommunications infrastructure in remote regions—likely contributing to underreporting.

According to Graeme Smith of the International Crisis Group: "The problem is that the drones are guided by less and less intelligence as the troops withdraw." He warns this scenario resembles conditions in Pakistan's Waziristan, where aerial operations struggle distinguishing between legitimate military targets and civilian gatherings.

The investigation relies on open-source information to create a strike-by-strike database, with plans to expand source diversity over time.
