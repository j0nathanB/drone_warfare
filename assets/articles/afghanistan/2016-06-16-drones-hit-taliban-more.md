title: US drones hit Taliban more than terrorist networks despite end of Afghan war
date: June 16, 2016
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reports that the majority of US airstrikes in Afghanistan during 2016 targeted ground support rather than suspected terrorist organizations. Over 200 strikes—predominantly by drones—were conducted to defend forces battling Taliban insurgents, representing more than 60% of all US airstrikes in the country.

**Key findings:**

- Since combat operations officially ended in 2014, US forces have maintained a "train, advise and assist" role under NATO's Resolute Support mission
- Between January-May 2016, 451 weapons were released compared to 189 during the same 2015 period
- Of 347 airstrikes in early 2016, 213 (61%) were described as defensive force-protection strikes
- In June 2016, Washington relaxed rules of engagement to explicitly permit proactive Taliban support operations

**Expert perspective:** Kate Clark of the Afghan Analyst Network characterized the shift as a pragmatic response to deteriorating security conditions, describing it as "having to say one thing and do another" regarding America's stated combat withdrawal.

The article notes controversial incidents, including the May 2016 drone strike killing Taliban leader Akhtar Mansour in Pakistan, justified as a defensive action.
