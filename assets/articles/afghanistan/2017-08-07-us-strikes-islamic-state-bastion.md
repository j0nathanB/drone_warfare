title: Afghanistan air war: US strikes pound Islamic State bastion
date: August 7, 2017
authors: Jack Serle, Jessica Purkiss
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism obtained detailed breakdown data on US military strikes across Afghan provinces for the first time, revealing operational patterns in the air campaign.

**Key Findings:**

Nearly half of July's 358 strikes targeted Nangarhar province in eastern Afghanistan, where Islamic State - Khorasan (IS-K) maintains its strongest presence. "Strikes in the province overtook those carried out in Helmand - traditionally one of the fiercest fronts of the Afghan war."

IS-K numbers approximately 1,000 fighters across five provinces despite a March offensive launched jointly by US and Afghan forces. The group has proven resilient despite sustained military pressure.

**Strike Categorization:**

Force-protection strikes—defensive air operations called when troops face immediate threats—surged dramatically. These increased "nearly 90% from 14 in June to more than 101 in July," coinciding with three NATO soldier casualties from Taliban attacks that month.

Strategic effects strikes dominated overall operations, supporting Afghan military objectives including town and city defense. In Nangarhar specifically, nearly two-thirds of attacks fell into this category.

**Regional Operations:**

US strikes extended beyond Afghanistan, with operations confirmed in Yemen and Somalia targeting al Qaeda and al-Shabaab personnel during July 2017.
