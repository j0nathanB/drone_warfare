title: US response further confuses controversial Kunduz strike
date: November 15, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Ruhullah Khapalwak, Abigail Fielding-Smith
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported on contradictions in US military statements regarding a November 4, 2017 airstrike in Kunduz province, Afghanistan.

**Key Points:**

- Resolute Support initially claimed "no evidence of civilian casualties," though subsequent evidence contradicted this position.

- The UN investigation suggested at least 10 civilians were killed, with hospital records confirming six injured civilians admitted from the strike.

- A NATO spokesperson later provided a statement acknowledging that "civilians could be mistaken as members of the insurgency based on their offensive activities," particularly when coerced into collecting bodies.

- The Bureau compiled a verified list of 10 deceased civilians, all confirmed by multiple sources, with ages ranging from teenagers to 70.

- A survivor, Muhammad Saeed (22), described the attack: "The intensity of the bombing was so strong that we felt an earth quake." He reported witnessing armed men forcing villagers to collect bodies before the strike occurred.

- If confirmed, this would represent "one of the biggest mistakes US aircraft have made in Afghanistan this year."

The article highlights significant inconsistencies between official military claims and on-the-ground evidence from medical facilities and survivors.
