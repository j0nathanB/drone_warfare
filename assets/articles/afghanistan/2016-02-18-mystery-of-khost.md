title: The Mystery of Khost: Did a US drone kill 14 innocent Afghans as they prepared a funeral?
date: February 18, 2016
authors: Abigail Fielding-Smith, Payenda Sargand, Jack Serle
content:

The article investigates a June 5, 2015 drone strike in Khost, Afghanistan. The investigation examines whether a US drone strike killed 14 innocent civilians who were preparing for a funeral ceremony.

The piece is part of The Bureau of Investigative Journalism's Drone Warfare project, which tracks and analyzes counterterrorism operations. The report was co-published with major media outlets to maximize reach.

**Author Backgrounds:**
- Fielding-Smith reports on counterterrorism and international affairs; formerly FT Beirut correspondent
- Sargand is an Afghanistan expert and long-time Bureau consultant
- Serle has covered drones and counterterrorism for five years, managing the Bureau's drone warfare datasets

The full investigative feature examines "what happened in Khost on June 5 2015," analyzing civilian casualties from the airstrike.
