title: Afghan air war hits seven year high
date: October 6, 2017
authors: Jack Serle, Jessica Purkiss
content:

### Main Report

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported that American military aircraft released more weapons in September 2017 than in any month since November 2010. According to US Air Forces Central Command data, 751 weapons were deployed during that period.

The authors note that "weapons released" differs from airstrike counts, as individual strikes can involve multiple weapons. The intensity of operations had escalated significantly, with the US completing more strikes by October 2017 than during the entire preceding two years combined.

### Data Correction Notice

The article includes a critical update: AFCENT subsequently revised the September figure from 751 to 414 weapons, making the original reporting inaccurate. The correction indicated that August—not September—represented the peak, with 503 weapons released. This marked the highest monthly total since August 2012.

AFCENT attributed the error to miscounting 20mm rounds fired by F-16 jets, explaining they had counted individual rounds rather than grouping every 100 rounds as one weapon.

### Context

General Nicholson had announced in August that air support to Afghan forces would increase following President Trump's new strategic approach. Defense Secretary James Mattis confirmed international forces conducted more strikes under this new administration than any year since 2012.
