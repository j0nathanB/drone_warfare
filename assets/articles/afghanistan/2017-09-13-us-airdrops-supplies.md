title: US airdrops supplies to Afghanistan as security worsens
date: September 13, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss
content:

The US military conducted its initial combat airdrop in Afghanistan following a gap exceeding two years, according to newly released Air Force documentation from August 2017.

A C-130J Super Hercules transport aircraft delivered more than five tonnes of equipment to coalition forces. While specific location details were unavailable, an Air Force spokesperson confirmed the drop occurred at "an established location that has experienced indirect fire in the past." This resupply method had remained unnecessary since at least 2014.

The frequency of airdrops declined sharply beginning in 2014 and ceased entirely during 2015-2016. The August operation delivered over 12,000 pounds—representing nearly half the total supplies dropped throughout 2014.

The resumption of airdrops reflects deteriorating security conditions. This delivery method minimizes aircraft exposure in hostile environments by avoiding runway landings.

Following President Trump's August 2017 speech, additional US troops arrived in Afghanistan. Air Force data revealed that August 2017 saw more weapons released on Afghanistan "than in any other single month since 2012."
