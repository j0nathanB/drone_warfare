title: US military operations against Taliban dramatically escalate
date: May 25, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss
content:

US military operations targeting the Taliban—nominally concluded in December 2014—experienced significant expansion during the Trump administration's early months. Data obtained by The Bureau reveals that "over 70% of the 898 strikes carried out since the start of the year until the end of April have targeted the group."

The escalation intensified in April, when operations surpassed levels not seen since August 2012. Taliban forces absorbed 296 of these strikes. Prior rule changes from June 2016 expanded commander authority to engage the group, though Afghan forces continue struggling to maintain territorial control.

Simultaneously, US forces deployed against the Islamic State's Afghan branch, deploying the GBU-43/B Massive Ordnance Air Blast bomb on April 13 to target tunnel systems in Nangarhar province. Defense Secretary Jim Mattis indicated 2017 would present "a tough year" for international forces.

General John Nicholson, the US commander in Afghanistan, requested additional NATO personnel in February, describing the situation as a "stalemate" requiring reinforcement. The Trump administration awaited decision on this troop request.
