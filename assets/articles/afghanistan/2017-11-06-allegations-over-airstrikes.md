title: Allegations levelled against US military over airstrikes in Afghanistan
date: November 6, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Ruhullah Khapalwak
content:

The US military is investigating claims that airstrikes in Kunduz province, Afghanistan killed numerous civilians. The precise death toll remains contested among various sources.

**The Incident:**
Aerial bombardment intensified on November 3 across multiple villages in Chardara district during ongoing operations against Taliban forces. A tribal elder described the intensity: "everyone believed that the whole Chardara district must be levelled and destroyed."

**Conflicting Casualty Estimates:**
Reports vary significantly regarding civilian deaths:
- Provincial council member: 55 civilians killed
- Tribal elder: 29 civilians killed
- District deputy police chief: "small number" of civilians
- Provincial governor's spokesman: 1 civilian dead, 6 injured
- Afghan commando forces: no civilian deaths

**Disputed Circumstances:**
Sources disagree on how civilians became caught in the crossfire. Some accounts suggest Taliban forced locals to collect insurgent bodies, after which US forces struck again. Others indicate civilians returned to assess damage from the previous night's bombing. A third account describes the strike hitting a vehicle evacuating Taliban casualties.

**Context:**
Kunduz has experienced other significant civilian casualties from airstrikes, including a 2015 hospital strike killing 42 people and a 2009 NATO strike killing at least 90 civilians.
