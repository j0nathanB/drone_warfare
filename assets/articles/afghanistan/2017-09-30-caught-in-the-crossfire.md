title: Caught in the crossfire
date: September 30, 2017
authors: Jack Serle, Ruhullah Khapalwak
content:

This investigative report documents three airstrikes in Afghanistan's Helmand province during February 2017 that killed 26 civilians, including 19 children and 5 women.

### Key Events

The article centers on Hamid Gul, an 18-year-old shopkeeper whose family was killed when their home in Sangin was destroyed. His mother and seven siblings—all under 16—died in the strike on February 9, 2017. Two additional houses were hit on February 10 and 11, resulting in further civilian casualties.

### Investigation Findings

Local officials and the UN attributed the strikes to US airstrikes, noting "there appeared to have been no fighting in the area at the time." However, Resolute Support (the NATO mission) stated it "could not confirm or deny responsibility" after four investigations, classifying the case as "disputed."

### Policy Context

The investigation reveals how policy shifts affected civilian casualty rates. Following stricter rules implemented by General McChrystal in 2008, civilian deaths declined dramatically. However, after June 2016, when targeting rules expanded to help Afghan forces hold territory, airstrike frequency increased substantially.

### Access Challenges

A senior US military official explained the difficulty: "Not only do we not have access, as the military" but aid organizations also lack "access they once had...so it's much harder to really dig into an allegation."

### Aftermath

The affected families received limited compensation while awaiting government decisions on full reparations. The article underscores persistent accountability gaps in America's longest-running conflict.
