title: Afghan airstrikes killed and injured record number of civilians in 2016
date: February 6, 2017
authors: Jack Serle
content:

The piece reports that airstrikes in Afghanistan caused unprecedented civilian casualties in 2016. According to UN data, air attacks killed 250 and injured 340 civilians—nearly double the 2015 figure. The conflict overall resulted in over 11,000 civilian casualties, with 3,512 being children, marking the highest recorded toll since systematic UN monitoring began in 2009.

**Key findings:**
- US forces caused 40% of airstrike casualties; Afghan Air Force caused 43%
- Strike frequency increased 50% from 2015 to 2016
- US pilots killed or injured one civilian per three strikes on average
- More than half of civilian casualties concentrated in Kunduz, Helmand, and Nangarhar provinces

**Context provided:** The Taliban's resurgence forced greater reliance on air support. NATO operations dwindled post-2014, compelling the US to conduct counterinsurgency largely through aerial bombardment. A June rule change permitted specifically targeting the Taliban, previously restricted to al Qaeda and ISIS.

The UN called for "an immediate halt to the use of airstrikes in civilian-populated areas," citing the devastating humanitarian impact across Afghanistan's conflict zones.
