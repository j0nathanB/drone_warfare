title: Fewer strikes - but more bombs hit Afghanistan
date: October 13, 2017
authors: Jessica Purkiss, Jack Serle
content:

The Bureau of Investigative Journalism reported on US air operations in Afghanistan during September 2017. Despite conducting fewer strikes than in previous months, American forces deployed significantly more ordnance.

**Key Findings:**

The US carried out 212 strikes while "launched 751 weapons – an average of 3.5 weapons dropped per strike." This represented a substantial increase in munitions deployed compared to August, when approximately one weapon per strike was released.

**Explanation:**

Increased B-52 bomber missions accounted for the higher weapons count. These strategic aircraft "can carry more than 30 tonnes of the widest array of weapons in the US inventory, dropping multiple munitions at a time."

The analysis distinguished between strikes and weapons released, as NATO's Resolute Support mission counts multiple ordnance drops on a single target as one strike.

**Correction Note:**

The Bureau disclosed that original Air Force Central Command data was later revised. Initial figures reported 751 weapons but were corrected to 414 in October due to miscounting 20mm rounds from F-16 jets.
